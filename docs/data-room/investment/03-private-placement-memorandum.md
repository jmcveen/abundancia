## 3. Description of the Company

### 3.1 Formation and Purpose

Abundancia Austin Holdings, LLC (the "Company") is a Texas Series Limited Liability Company formed under the Texas Business Organizations Code for the purpose of acquiring, developing, and operating a 376-acre regenerative master-planned community in Cedar Creek, Bastrop County, Texas.

### 3.2 Business Objectives

The Company's primary business objectives are:

(a) **Acquire** approximately 376 acres of undeveloped land in Cedar Creek, Bastrop County, Texas, along the State Highway 71 corridor;

(b) **Develop** the property as a regenerative master-planned community featuring hempcrete residential construction, regenerative agriculture, and community-centered design;

(c) **Construct and sell** single-family homes, tiny homes, geodesic dome residences, multifamily units, and finished building lots;

(d) **Retain and operate** a portfolio of rental units, commercial spaces, and a retreat/event center for recurring income;

(e) **Implement** regenerative land management practices including food forests, native habitat restoration, and carbon sequestration programs;

(f) **Generate** attractive risk-adjusted returns for investors through a diversified revenue model spanning residential sales, rental income, lot sales, commercial leases, and retreat/event revenue; and

(g) **Establish** a replicable model for regenerative community development that may be deployed in additional markets.

## 8. Distribution Waterfall

### 8.1 Distribution Priority

Available cash flow from operations and capital events (sales, refinancing, dissolution) shall be distributed in the following order of priority:

**Tier 1 — Return of Capital (100% to LPs)**

First, 100% to the Limited Partners until each Limited Partner has received cumulative distributions equal to its aggregate Capital Contributions.

**Tier 2 — Preferred Return (100% to LPs)**

Second, 100% to the Limited Partners until each Limited Partner has received a cumulative annual return equal to 8% of its unreturned Capital Contribution, compounded annually from the date of each Capital Contribution (the "Preferred Return").

**Tier 3 — GP Catch-Up (80% LP / 20% GP)**

Third, 80% to the Limited Partners and 20% to the General Partner, until the General Partner has received cumulative distributions equal to 20% of the aggregate amount distributed under Tiers 2 and 3.

**Tier 4 — Above 15% IRR (70% LP / 30% GP)**

Fourth, after the Limited Partners have achieved a 15% internal rate of return on their Capital Contributions, 70% to the Limited Partners and 30% to the General Partner.

**Tier 5 — Above 25% IRR (60% LP / 40% GP)**

Fifth, after the Limited Partners have achieved a 25% internal rate of return on their Capital Contributions, 60% to the Limited Partners and 40% to the General Partner.

**Tier 6 — Above 35% IRR (50% LP / 50% GP)**

Sixth, after the Limited Partners have achieved a 35% internal rate of return on their Capital Contributions, 50% to the Limited Partners and 50% to the General Partner.

### 8.2 Waterfall Summary Table

| Tier | Threshold | LP Share | GP Share | Description |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Until 1.0x return of capital | 100% | 0% | Return of invested capital |
| 2 | Until 8% preferred return | 100% | 0% | Cumulative preferred return |
| 3 | Catch-up | 80% | 20% | GP catch-up to 20% of Tier 2-3 |
| 4 | Above 15% IRR | 70% | 30% | Performance tier |
| 5 | Above 25% IRR | 60% | 40% | Enhanced performance tier |
| 6 | Above 35% IRR | 50% | 50% | Maximum performance tier |

### 8.3 Distribution Timing

The Manager intends to make distributions quarterly, subject to available cash flow and the maintenance of adequate reserves for operations, debt service, and anticipated capital expenditures. The Manager has sole discretion over the timing and amount of distributions, and there can be no assurance that distributions will be made on any particular schedule or at all.

### 8.4 Clawback

In the event that, upon final dissolution and liquidation of the Company, the General Partner has received cumulative distributions in excess of the amounts it would have been entitled to under the waterfall based on the Company's total performance, the General Partner shall return such excess to the Limited Partners (the "Clawback Obligation"). This ensures that the waterfall operates on a whole-fund basis, not a deal-by-deal basis.

**Real Estate Market Cycles.** Real estate markets are cyclical and subject to periods of expansion and contraction. A downturn in the Austin residential market could reduce demand for the Company's products, decrease achievable sale prices, and adversely impact financial performance. Historical data does not guarantee future market conditions.

**Interest Rate Risk.** Rising interest rates may reduce the pool of qualified homebuyers by increasing mortgage costs, slow residential sales velocity, and increase the Company's cost of construction financing. Each 100 basis point increase in mortgage rates reduces buyer purchasing power by approximately 10%.

**Competition.** The Austin MSA is an active market for residential development. New master-planned communities, conventional subdivisions, and infill projects compete for the same buyer pool. There can be no assurance that the Company's differentiated positioning will sustain pricing premiums throughout the development period.

**Pricing Risk.** The financial projections assume specific pricing for each product type. Actual achievable prices may be lower than projected due to market conditions, competition, or other factors. A 10% reduction in achievable prices would reduce projected revenue by approximately $43.5 million.

### 9.3 Construction Risks

**Cost Overruns.** Construction costs may exceed projections due to material price increases, labor shortages, design changes, unforeseen site conditions, or contractor performance issues. While the Company has allocated 18% of proceeds to contingency, cost overruns exceeding this reserve would require additional capital.

**Hempcrete Construction Risk.** While hempcrete is a proven building material internationally, the domestic hempcrete construction industry is less mature than conventional construction. The Company may face challenges sourcing qualified hempcrete contractors, obtaining consistent material supply, and achieving projected construction timelines.

**Labor Market.** The Central Texas construction labor market has experienced persistent tightness. Difficulty obtaining qualified labor could delay construction timelines and increase costs.

**Material Supply.** The Company's construction program depends on reliable supply of hemp hurd, lime binder, and other materials. Supply disruptions, whether due to agricultural conditions, trade policy, or logistics, could delay construction or increase costs.

**Schedule Risk.** Development timelines are estimates and may be extended due to permitting delays, weather events, labor shortages, material delivery delays, or other factors. Delays increase carrying costs and defer revenue, adversely impacting returns.

### 9.4 Environmental Risks

**Natural Disasters.** Bastrop County has a documented history of wildfire risk, including the 2011 Bastrop County Complex Fire. While hempcrete's superior fire rating (2+ hours) provides structural protection, wildfire events could damage infrastructure, displace residents, and adversely impact property values and sales velocity.

**Drought.** Central Texas is subject to periodic drought conditions that may affect water availability, agricultural productivity, and landscape aesthetics. Extended drought could impact the Company's regenerative agriculture program and community amenities.

**Flood Risk.** While the property is predominantly in Zone X (minimal flood hazard), climate change may alter historical precipitation patterns and flood risk. Flash flooding on or near the property could damage infrastructure or delay construction.

**Environmental Contamination.** Although no environmental contamination is currently known or suspected, undiscovered contamination could require costly remediation and delay development.

### 9.5 Regulatory Risks

**Changes in Law.** Changes in federal, state, or local laws, regulations, tax policy, or building codes could increase development costs, restrict the development program, or adversely impact the financial viability of the project. While Bastrop County currently has no county-level zoning, this policy could change.

**Permitting Delays.** Although Bastrop County's permitting environment is favorable compared to Travis County, permitting delays may still occur due to agency capacity, public opposition, or regulatory changes.

**Securities Law.** The Company is relying on exemptions from securities registration under Regulation D, Rule 506(c). Failure to comply with applicable securities laws could result in rescission rights for investors, penalties, and other adverse consequences.

**Environmental Regulation.** Federal and state environmental regulations (including TCEQ requirements) may impose obligations or restrictions on the Company's development activities, including wastewater treatment, stormwater management, and habitat protection.

### 9.6 Liquidity Risk

**No Public Market.** There is no public market for the Units, and none is expected to develop. The Units are subject to significant transfer restrictions under the Operating Agreement and applicable securities laws. Investors should be prepared to hold their investment for the full 7-10 year project term.

**Limited Redemption.** The Company is not obligated to redeem or repurchase Units from investors. While the Manager may, in its discretion, facilitate transfers between investors, there can be no assurance that any secondary market or transfer opportunity will be available.

### 9.7 Concentration Risk

**Single Asset.** The Company's investment is concentrated in a single 376-acre property in a single geographic market. Investors have no diversification across properties, markets, or asset classes within this investment. Adverse conditions affecting the property, Bastrop County, or the Austin MSA would disproportionately impact the investment.

### 9.8 Capital Risk

**Additional Capital.** The Company may require additional capital beyond the proceeds of this offering. There can be no assurance that additional financing will be available on acceptable terms or at all. Failure to obtain adequate financing could result in reduced project scope, delays, or termination.

**Leverage.** The Company intends to utilize construction financing and potentially MUD bond debt, which will increase the Company's overall leverage. High leverage amplifies both gains and losses, and the Company's ability to service debt obligations depends on the project generating sufficient cash flow.

### 9.9 Key Person Risk

**Dependence on Key Personnel.** The Company's success depends significantly on the continued involvement of Nicholas Courchesne, Jason Sparks, and other key personnel. The loss or disability of any key person could adversely affect the Company's ability to execute its business plan.

### 9.10 Tax Risks

**Tax Law Changes.** Federal and state tax laws are subject to change, and such changes could adversely affect the tax benefits available to investors, including the treatment of partnership income, depreciation deductions, and potential Opportunity Zone benefits.

**IRS Audit.** The Company's tax returns may be audited by the Internal Revenue Service or state tax authorities. An adverse determination in an audit could result in additional taxes, penalties, and interest for investors.

## 10. Investor Suitability Standards

### 10.1 Accredited Investor Requirement

The Units are being offered exclusively to "accredited investors" as defined in Rule 501(a) of Regulation D under the Securities Act of 1933, as amended. Under Rule 506(c), the Company must take reasonable steps to verify that each investor is an accredited investor.

### 10.2 Accredited Investor Definition

An "accredited investor" includes, among others:

**(a) Natural Persons** who meet at least one of the following criteria:

(i) Individual annual income exceeding $200,000 (or joint income with spouse/spousal equivalent exceeding $300,000) in each of the two most recent years, with a reasonable expectation of reaching the same income level in the current year;

(ii) Individual net worth (or joint net worth with spouse/spousal equivalent) exceeding $1,000,000, excluding the value of the primary residence;

(iii) Holder of a Series 7, Series 65, or Series 82 license in good standing; or

(iv) "Knowledgeable employee" of a private fund (as defined under the Investment Company Act).

**(b) Entities** meeting certain criteria, including:

(i) Any trust with total assets exceeding $5,000,000, not formed for the specific purpose of investing in the Company, whose purchase is directed by a person with knowledge and experience in financial matters;

(ii) Any entity in which all equity owners are accredited investors;

(iii) Any entity with total assets exceeding $5,000,000; or

(iv) Certain institutional investors (banks, insurance companies, registered investment companies, etc.).

### 10.3 Verification Methods

Pursuant to Rule 506(c), the Company will verify accredited investor status through one or more of the following methods:

- Review of tax returns (W-2, 1099, K-1, Form 1040) for the two most recent years
- Review of bank, brokerage, or other financial statements
- Written confirmation from a registered broker-dealer, SEC-registered investment adviser, licensed CPA, or licensed attorney that the investor is accredited
- Third-party verification service
- Existing investor certification (for investors who previously invested in the Company's offerings within the preceding 5 years, provided no changes to accredited status)

### 10.4 Additional Suitability Considerations

In addition to meeting the accredited investor standard, prospective investors should consider whether this investment is suitable for them based on:

- Their overall financial situation and the percentage of portfolio allocated to illiquid investments
- Their ability to bear the economic risk of a total loss of the investment

## 11. Tax Considerations

### 11.1 Partnership Taxation

The Company intends to be treated as a partnership for federal income tax purposes. As a partnership, the Company itself does not pay federal income tax. Instead, each investor will be allocated a pro rata share of the Company's income, gain, loss, deduction, and credit, which will be reported on the investor's individual tax return.

### 11.2 Schedule K-1

The Company will provide each investor with an annual Schedule K-1 (Form 1065) reflecting the investor's allocable share of partnership items for the tax year. K-1s will be provided on or before March 15 of the following year (or as soon thereafter as practicable).

### 11.3 Tax Benefits

Investors may benefit from the following tax attributes of the investment:

**(a) Depreciation.** Investors will receive their pro rata share of depreciation deductions attributable to the Company's depreciable real property and personal property. Depreciation deductions may offset income from other sources, subject to passive activity loss limitation rules.

**(b) Pass-Through Losses.** In the early development phases, the Company may generate tax losses that pass through to investors, potentially offsetting taxable income from other sources. The deductibility of such losses is subject to the passive activity loss rules, at-risk rules, and basis limitations.

**(c) Section 199A Deduction.** Investors may be eligible for the Section 199A deduction (qualified business income deduction) on their share of qualifying partnership income, subject to applicable limitations and phase-outs.

**(d) 1031 Exchange Potential.** The Company may utilize Section 1031 like-kind exchanges to defer gain on the disposition of real property, preserving capital for reinvestment.

### 11.4 Opportunity Zone Potential

The Company is evaluating whether the property is located in, or adjacent to, a federally designated Qualified Opportunity Zone under the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017. If Opportunity Zone benefits are available, investors may be eligible for:

- Temporary deferral of capital gains invested in the Opportunity Zone fund
- Potential step-up in basis for gains held 5+ years
- Potential exclusion of gain on appreciation of the Opportunity Zone investment if held 10+ years

**Note:** Opportunity Zone benefits have not been confirmed for this investment. Investors should not rely on Opportunity Zone treatment in making their investment decision.

### 11.5 State Taxes

Investors may be subject to Texas franchise tax obligations depending on their entity structure and filing status. Texas does not impose a personal income tax. However, investors residing in other states may be subject to their home state's income tax on their share of Company income.
