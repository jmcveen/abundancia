## 1. Subscription and Purchase

### 1.1 Subscription

The Subscriber hereby irrevocably subscribes for and agrees to purchase the number of Units set forth on the signature page hereof (the "Subscribed Units") at a purchase price of Fifty Thousand Dollars ($50,000.00) per Unit (the "Purchase Price"), for an aggregate subscription amount as set forth on the signature page hereof (the "Subscription Amount").

### 1.2 The Offering

The Company is offering up to $12,500,000 in Class A Limited Partnership Interests (the "Offering") pursuant to Section 4(a)(2) of the Securities Act and Rule 506(c) of Regulation D promulgated thereunder. The Offering is limited exclusively to persons who are "accredited investors" as defined in Rule 501(a) of Regulation D. General solicitation and general advertising are permitted under Rule 506(c), provided that the Company takes reasonable steps to verify each investor's accredited investor status.

### 1.3 Minimum Investment

The minimum subscription amount is Fifty Thousand Dollars ($50,000) per Subscriber (one Unit). The Manager may, in its sole discretion, accept subscriptions for lesser amounts or fractional Units from certain investors.

### 1.4 Acceptance and Rejection

(a) This subscription is subject to acceptance by the Manager in its sole and absolute discretion. The Manager may accept or reject this subscription, in whole or in part, for any reason or no reason, without liability to the Subscriber.

(b) The Manager shall notify the Subscriber of acceptance or rejection within ten (10) business days of receipt of this fully executed Agreement together with the Subscription Amount and all required verification documentation.

(c) If this subscription is rejected, the Subscription Amount shall be returned to the Subscriber within five (5) business days of rejection, without interest.

(d) If this subscription is accepted, the Subscriber shall receive written confirmation from the Manager, together with an executed copy of this Agreement and the Operating Agreement.

### 1.5 Effective Date of Admission

Upon acceptance of this subscription by the Manager, the Subscriber shall be admitted as a Limited Partner of the Company effective as of the date of acceptance (the "Admission Date"). The Subscriber's rights to distributions and allocations of income, gain, loss, deduction, and credit shall commence as of the Admission Date and shall be prorated for any partial period in accordance with the Operating Agreement.

### 1.6 Irrevocability

This subscription, once submitted, is irrevocable by the Subscriber except as required by applicable law. The Subscriber acknowledges and agrees that it may not withdraw or revoke this subscription after the Subscription Amount has been transmitted to the Company's escrow account.

### 1.7 Minimum Offering Amount

(a) The Company has established a minimum offering amount of Five Million Dollars ($5,000,000) (the "Minimum Offering Amount"). No investor funds will be deployed for any purpose (including land acquisition) until the Minimum Offering Amount has been achieved.

(b) All subscription funds will be held in an interest-free escrow account at a federally insured depository institution until the Minimum Offering Amount is reached.

(c) If the Minimum Offering Amount is not achieved by September 30, 2026 (or such later date as the Manager may determine in its sole discretion, but in no event later than December 31, 2026), all escrowed funds shall be returned to Subscribers, without interest, and this Agreement shall be void and of no further force or effect.

### 1.8 Rolling Closings

The Manager may conduct multiple closings (each, a "Closing") at its discretion after the Minimum Offering Amount has been achieved. Investors whose subscriptions are accepted in subsequent Closings will be admitted as Limited Partners effective as of their respective Closing dates, with appropriate pro rata adjustments to distributions, allocations, and preferred return calculations.

## 5. Risk Acknowledgments

### 5.1 General Risk Acknowledgment

The Subscriber acknowledges that an investment in the Units involves a high degree of risk, including all of the risks described in Section 9 ("Risk Factors") of the Private Placement Memorandum (Document 03). ### 5.2 Specific Risk Acknowledgments

Without limiting the generality of Section 5.1, the Subscriber specifically acknowledges, understands, and accepts the following risks:

**(a) Illiquidity and No Public Market.** There is no public market for the Units, and none is expected to develop. The Units are subject to significant transfer restrictions under this Agreement, the Operating Agreement, and applicable securities laws. The Subscriber may not be able to sell, transfer, or otherwise dispose of the Units for an indefinite period and should be prepared to hold the investment for the full anticipated 7-10 year project term, or longer. Forced or distressed sale of the Units would likely result in a significant discount to the Units' intrinsic value.

**(b) Loss of Entire Investment.** The Subscriber may lose the entire Subscription Amount. The Company is a development-stage entity with no operating history, no current revenue, and no assets (other than cash). There is no guarantee of returns, distributions, or return of capital.

**(c) Forward-Looking Statements.** The Offering Documents contain forward-looking statements, projections, pro forma financial information, and estimates that are inherently uncertain and based on assumptions that may not prove to be correct. Actual results may differ materially and adversely from the projections set forth in the Offering Documents.

**(d) Pre-Development Stage.** The Company is in a pre-acquisition, pre-development stage. The Company has not yet acquired the property, obtained any entitlements or permits, commenced any construction, or generated any revenue. The Company has no operating history upon which to evaluate its likely performance.

**(e) Single-Asset Concentration.** The investment is concentrated in a single 376-acre property in Bastrop County, Texas. There is no diversification across properties, geographic markets, or asset classes. Adverse conditions affecting the property, Bastrop County, the Austin metropolitan area, or the Texas economy would disproportionately impact the investment.

**(f) Management Control.** The Manager has broad discretionary authority over all aspects of the Company's operations, including the deployment of capital, the timing and scope of development, the selection of contractors and service providers, the pricing and marketing of units, and the timing and amount of distributions. Limited Partners have limited voting rights and limited ability to influence or control management decisions.

**(g) Hempcrete Construction Risk.** While hempcrete is a proven building material with centuries of use internationally, the domestic hempcrete construction industry is less mature than conventional construction. The Company may experience challenges in sourcing qualified hempcrete contractors, obtaining consistent and affordable material supply, achieving projected construction costs and timelines, and obtaining building code approvals in all phases.

**(h) Natural Disaster Risk.** Bastrop County has a documented history of wildfire risk, including the 2011 Bastrop County Complex Fire that destroyed over 1,600 homes. The property may also be subject to drought, flooding, severe weather, and other natural disasters that could damage the property, disrupt construction, displace residents, and adversely impact property values, sales velocity, and returns.

**(i) Regulatory and Legal Risk.** Changes in federal, state, or local laws, regulations, building codes, environmental requirements, or tax policy could increase development costs, restrict the planned development program, or adversely impact the financial viability of the project. The favorable regulatory environment in Bastrop County (no county zoning, streamlined permitting) could change.

**(j) Tax Risk.** The tax consequences of the investment are complex and may vary significantly based on each investor's individual circumstances. Federal and state tax laws are subject to change, and such changes could adversely affect the tax benefits described in the Offering Documents. The Subscriber may owe taxes on allocations of Company income even in years when no cash distributions are received. The Subscriber has been advised to consult with the Subscriber's own tax advisor.

**(k) Conflict of Interest.** The General Partner and its principals receive compensation (management fees, acquisition fees, construction management fees, disposition fees, and carried interest) that creates potential conflicts of interest between the Manager and the Limited Partners. The Manager's incentive to deploy capital and proceed with development may not always align with the interests of the Limited Partners.

## 7. Distribution Rights

### 7.1 Distribution Entitlement

Subject to the terms of the Operating Agreement, the Subscriber shall be entitled to receive distributions of available cash flow from the Company's operations and capital events (sales, refinancings, and dissolution proceeds) in accordance with the distribution waterfall described in Section 8 of the Private Placement Memorandum and Article [VIII] of the Operating Agreement.

### 7.2 Distribution Waterfall Summary

Distributions shall be made in the following order of priority:

| Tier | Description | LP Share | GP Share | Threshold |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Return of Capital | 100% | 0% | Until 1.0x return of contributed capital |
| **2** | Preferred Return | 100% | 0% | Until 8% cumulative annual return achieved |
| **3** | GP Catch-Up | 80% | 20% | Until GP receives 20% of Tier 2-3 distributions |
| **4** | Performance Tier I | 70% | 30% | After LP achieves 15% IRR |
| **5** | Performance Tier II | 60% | 40% | After LP achieves 25% IRR |
| **6** | Performance Tier III | 50% | 50% | After LP achieves 35% IRR |

### 7.3 Preferred Return

The preferred return shall accrue at a rate of eight percent (8%) per annum on the Subscriber's unreturned capital contributions, compounding annually from the date of each capital contribution. The preferred return is cumulative; any unpaid preferred return carries forward and must be fully satisfied before the General Partner participates in any distributions above Tier 2.

### 7.4 Distribution Timing and Discretion

(a) The Manager intends to make distributions on a quarterly basis, subject to available cash flow and the maintenance of adequate reserves for operations, debt service, capital expenditures, and contingencies.

(b) The timing and amount of all distributions are in the sole discretion of the Manager. THERE CAN BE NO ASSURANCE THAT DISTRIBUTIONS WILL BE MADE ON ANY PARTICULAR SCHEDULE OR AT ALL.

(c) The Manager anticipates that the first distributions will commence approximately 18-24 months after the initial closing (estimated Q1 2028), subject to project performance and available cash flow.

### 7.5 Tax Distributions

The Manager shall use commercially reasonable efforts to distribute to each Limited Partner, prior to the due date for quarterly estimated tax payments, an amount sufficient to cover the Limited Partner's estimated federal and state income tax liability attributable to the Limited Partner's allocable share of Company income for the relevant tax period (a "Tax Distribution"), calculated at the highest applicable individual marginal tax rate. Tax Distributions shall be treated as advances against the Subscriber's distribution entitlement under the waterfall and shall reduce subsequent distributions.

### 7.6 Withholding

The Company may withhold from distributions any amounts required to be withheld under applicable federal, state, or local tax laws. Amounts withheld shall be treated as distributions to the Subscriber for purposes of the distribution waterfall.
