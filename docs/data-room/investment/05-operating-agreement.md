## OPERATING AGREEMENT

## OF

## ABUNDANCIA AUSTIN HOLDINGS, LLC

## A TEXAS SERIES LIMITED LIABILITY COMPANY

---

THIS OPERATING AGREEMENT (this "Agreement") of **ABUNDANCIA AUSTIN HOLDINGS, LLC**, a Texas Series Limited Liability Company (the "Company"), is entered into and effective as of the date of the last signature hereto (the "Effective Date"), by and among:

**ABUNDANCIA AUSTIN GP, LLC**, a Texas limited liability company, as General Partner and Manager (the "Manager" or "General Partner" or "GP"); and

**THE PERSONS AND ENTITIES** listed on **Exhibit A** attached hereto, as Limited Partners (each a "Limited Partner" and collectively, the "Limited Partners" or "LPs");

(the Manager and the Limited Partners are collectively referred to herein as the "Members").

---

### RECITALS

A. The Company was formed as a Texas Series Limited Liability Company under the Texas Business Organizations Code (the "TBOC"), Title 3, Chapter 101, by the filing of a Certificate of Formation with the Secretary of State of Texas.

B. The Members desire to enter into this Agreement to set forth their respective rights, obligations, and duties with respect to the Company and to govern the management, operation, and affairs of the Company.

C. The Company intends to acquire, develop, and manage a 376-acre regenerative master-planned community located in Cedar Creek, Bastrop County, Texas (the "Project"), as more fully described in the Private Placement Memorandum dated March 2026 (the "Memorandum").

D. The Company is raising up to Twelve Million Five Hundred Thousand Dollars ($12,500,000) in equity capital through a Regulation D, Rule 506(c) offering of Class A Limited Partnership Interests to accredited investors.

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Members agree as follows:

## ARTICLE I: Formation and Organization

### Section 1.1 — Formation

The Company has been organized as a Texas Series Limited Liability Company pursuant to the provisions of the TBOC by the filing of a Certificate of Formation with the Texas Secretary of State. The rights and obligations of the Members shall be as provided in the TBOC, except as otherwise expressly provided herein. To the extent that the provisions of this Agreement are inconsistent with the non-mandatory provisions of the TBOC, this Agreement shall control.

### Section 1.2 — Name

The name of the Company is "Abundancia Austin Holdings, LLC." The Company's business shall be conducted under such name or under such other names as the Manager may from time to time designate, subject to compliance with applicable law.

### Section 1.3 — Series LLC Structure

(a) The Company is organized as a Series LLC under the TBOC, Section 101.601 et seq. The Manager is authorized to establish one or more separate series (each a "Series") within the Company, each of which may have separate assets, liabilities, members, and purposes.

(b) The following Series are hereby established:

| Series | Purpose | Assets |
|---|---|---|
| **Series A — Land Holdings** | Hold title to the 376-acre property and undeveloped land | Real property, mineral rights, easements |
| **Series B — Residential Development** | Develop, construct, and sell residential units and lots | Construction contracts, inventory, receivables |
| **Series C — Rental Portfolio** | Own and operate retained rental units | Rental properties, leases, operating accounts |
| **Series D — Commercial Operations** | Own and operate commercial spaces in the village center | Commercial leases, tenant improvements, operating accounts |
| **Series E — Retreat & Events** | Own and operate the retreat center and event programming | Retreat center property, event contracts, operating accounts |

(c) The debts, liabilities, obligations, and expenses incurred, contracted for, or otherwise existing with respect to a particular Series shall be enforceable only against the assets of such Series, and not against the assets of the Company generally or any other Series, to the fullest extent permitted by the TBOC.

(d) The Manager has sole authority to create additional Series, allocate assets and liabilities among Series, and dissolve or merge Series, in each case without the consent of the Limited Partners.

### Section 1.4 — Principal Office

The principal office of the Company shall be located in Austin, Texas, or at such other location as the Manager may from time to time designate. The Company may maintain additional offices at such places as the Manager deems necessary or appropriate.

### Section 1.5 — Registered Agent and Office

The registered agent and registered office of the Company in the State of Texas shall be as set forth in the Certificate of Formation, or as subsequently changed by the Manager in accordance with the TBOC.

### Section 1.6 — Term

The Company shall continue in existence until dissolved in accordance with Article XIII of this Agreement or as otherwise provided by law. The Company does not have a fixed term; however, the Manager anticipates that the primary development activities of the Project will span approximately ten (10) years from the date of initial land acquisition.

### Section 1.7 — Fiscal Year

The fiscal year of the Company shall be the calendar year ending December 31 of each year.

### Section 1.8 — Title to Property

All property owned by the Company, whether real, personal, or mixed, tangible or intangible, shall be deemed to be owned by the Company as an entity. No Member, individually, shall have any ownership interest in any Company property.

## ARTICLE II: Purpose and Powers

### Section 2.1 — Purpose

The Company is organized for the following purposes:

(a) To acquire approximately 376 acres of real property located in Cedar Creek, Bastrop County, Texas, along the State Highway 71 corridor;

(b) To develop, design, and master plan the property as a regenerative community featuring hempcrete residential construction, regenerative agriculture, renewable energy systems, and community-centered design;

(c) To construct, market, and sell single-family homes, tiny homes, geodesic dome residences, multifamily units, and finished building lots;

(d) To acquire, develop, and operate a portfolio of rental housing units for recurring income;

(e) To develop and operate commercial spaces, including a village center, retail, and co-working facilities;

(f) To develop and operate a retreat and event center;

(g) To implement regenerative land management practices, including food forests, native habitat restoration, regenerative agriculture, and carbon sequestration programs;

(h) To form or participate in the formation of Municipal Utility Districts (MUDs) or other special-purpose districts as may be advantageous for infrastructure financing;

(i) To obtain construction financing, permanent financing, and any other forms of debt or equity financing necessary or desirable for the development and operation of the Project;

(j) To enter into contracts, agreements, and arrangements necessary or incidental to the foregoing purposes; and

(k) To engage in any other lawful business activity related to or incidental to the foregoing purposes as the Manager may determine.

### Section 2.2 — Powers

The Company shall have and may exercise all powers and privileges granted to limited liability companies under the TBOC and any other applicable laws, together with any powers incidental thereto, so far as such powers and privileges are necessary or convenient for the conduct of the Company's business.

### Section 2.3 — Limitations

The Company shall not engage in any activity that is inconsistent with its purposes as set forth in Section 2.1 without the prior written approval of Members holding a Majority Interest (as defined herein).

## ARTICLE IV: Capital Contributions

### Section 4.1 — Initial Capital Contributions

(a) **General Partner.** The General Partner shall contribute to the Company the amount set forth on Exhibit A as its initial Capital Contribution.

(b) **Limited Partners.** Each Limited Partner shall contribute to the Company the Subscription Amount set forth in such Limited Partner's Subscription Agreement as its initial Capital Contribution. The minimum Capital Contribution for each Limited Partner is $50,000 (one Unit).

### Section 4.2 — Capital Call Authority

(a) The Manager may, from time to time, issue capital calls to the Limited Partners for additional Capital Contributions ("Additional Capital Contributions") if, in the Manager's reasonable judgment, additional capital is necessary to fund the Company's operations, development activities, debt service obligations, or other expenses.

(b) Capital calls shall be made by written notice to each Limited Partner at least thirty (30) days prior to the due date, specifying: (i) the aggregate amount of the capital call; (ii) each Limited Partner's pro rata share (based on Percentage Interest); (iii) the purpose of the capital call; and (iv) the due date for payment.

(c) Capital calls shall be proportional to each Limited Partner's Percentage Interest unless otherwise agreed by the affected Limited Partners.

### Section 4.3 — Default on Capital Calls

If a Limited Partner (a "Defaulting Member") fails to fund its pro rata share of a capital call within ten (10) days after the due date specified in the capital call notice, the Manager may, in its sole discretion, exercise one or more of the following remedies:

(a) **Dilution.** Reduce the Defaulting Member's Percentage Interest based on the ratio of the Defaulting Member's total funded Capital Contributions to the aggregate total funded Capital Contributions of all Members;

(b) **Default Interest.** Charge the Defaulting Member interest on the unfunded amount at a rate of eighteen percent (18%) per annum (or the maximum rate permitted by law, if lower) from the due date until payment;

(c) **Forfeiture of Preferred Return.** Suspend or forfeit the Defaulting Member's accrued preferred return on the unfunded amount;

(d) **Non-Contributing Member Loan.** Treat the unfunded amount as a loan from the Company to the Defaulting Member, bearing interest at eighteen percent (18%) per annum, repayable from the Defaulting Member's distributions; or

(e) **Forced Sale.** Require the Defaulting Member to sell its Units to the Company, the non-defaulting Members, or a third party, at a price equal to the lesser of: (i) seventy-five percent (75%) of the fair market value of the Units as determined by the Manager in good faith; or (ii) the Defaulting Member's unreturned Capital Contributions minus any outstanding default amounts.

### Section 4.4 — Return of Capital Contributions

(a) Except as expressly provided in Article VIII (Distributions) and Article XIII (Dissolution), no Member shall have the right to withdraw, reduce, or receive a return of its Capital Contribution, or to receive interest on its Capital Contribution.

(b) No Member shall be obligated to restore any deficit in its Capital Account upon dissolution or otherwise, except as may be required by the TBOC or this Agreement.

### Section 4.5 — No Additional Obligation

Except as provided in Section 4.2 (Capital Calls), no Member shall be required to contribute capital to the Company in excess of its initial Capital Contribution. A Limited Partner's liability is limited to the amount of its Capital Contribution and any obligation to fund capital calls, as set forth in this Agreement and the TBOC.

## ARTICLE V: Capital Accounts

### Section 5.1 — Maintenance of Capital Accounts

The Company shall establish and maintain a separate Capital Account for each Member in accordance with Treasury Regulation Section 1.704-1(b)(2)(iv) and the rules set forth in this Article V. The Capital Account of each Member shall be maintained as follows:

(a) **Credited** with: (i) the amount of cash contributed by such Member to the Company; (ii) the fair market value of property contributed by such Member (net of liabilities assumed or to which the property is subject); and (iii) allocations to such Member of Company income and gain (including tax-exempt income);

(b) **Debited** with: (i) the amount of cash distributed to such Member by the Company; (ii) the fair market value of property distributed to such Member (net of liabilities assumed or to which the property is subject); (iii) allocations to such Member of Company loss and deduction; and (iv) allocations to such Member of expenditures described in Section 705(a)(2)(B) of the Code.

### Section 5.2 — Adjustments

The Manager may, in its discretion, adjust the Capital Accounts of the Members to reflect a revaluation of Company property to its fair market value in connection with: (a) the contribution of money or other property to the Company by a new or existing Member; (b) the distribution of money or other property to a Member; or (c) the liquidation of the Company within the meaning of Treasury Regulation Section 1.704-1(b)(2)(ii)(g). Such adjustments shall be made in accordance with Treasury Regulation Section 1.704-1(b)(2)(iv)(f).

### Section 5.3 — Transferee Capital Accounts

Upon a Transfer of a Member's interest, the transferee shall succeed to the Capital Account of the transferor to the extent such Capital Account is attributable to the transferred interest.

### Section 5.4 — Negative Capital Accounts

No Member shall be required to pay to the Company or to any other Member any deficit balance in such Member's Capital Account. A negative Capital Account balance shall not constitute a debt or obligation of the Member to the Company or to any other Member.

### Section 5.5 — Compliance with Treasury Regulations

The provisions of this Article V are intended to comply with Treasury Regulation Section 1.704-1(b) and shall be interpreted and applied in a manner consistent with such Regulations. If the Manager determines that it is necessary or prudent to modify the manner in which Capital Accounts are computed to comply with such Regulations, the Manager may make such modifications, provided that such modifications are not likely to have a material adverse effect on the amounts distributable to any Member.

(b) Merger, consolidation, or conversion of the Company into another entity;

(c) Voluntary dissolution of the Company prior to the completion of the Project;

### Section 6.9 — Removal of Manager

(a) The Manager may be removed for "Cause" by the affirmative vote of Limited Partners holding at least seventy-five percent (75%) of the outstanding Class A Units. For purposes of this Section, "Cause" means:

   (i) Fraud, embezzlement, willful misconduct, or gross negligence by the Manager in the management of the Company's affairs that results in material harm to the Company;

   (ii) Material breach of this Agreement by the Manager that remains uncured for thirty (30) days after written notice from Limited Partners holding a Majority Interest, specifying the nature of the breach and the cure required;

   (iii) Conviction of the Manager (or any principal thereof) of a felony involving moral turpitude, dishonesty, or financial crime;

   (iv) Bankruptcy, insolvency, or dissolution of the Manager entity;

   (v) Misappropriation or conversion of Company funds or assets; or

   (vi) Failure to provide financial reports as required by Article XII for two (2) or more consecutive quarters, without reasonable justification.

(b) Upon removal of the Manager for Cause, the Limited Partners holding a Supermajority Interest shall appoint a successor Manager within ninety (90) days.

(c) Upon removal for Cause, the Manager's right to receive future management fees, acquisition fees, construction management fees, and disposition fees shall terminate as of the date of removal. The Manager shall retain its Carried Interest with respect to distributions attributable to investment activities occurring prior to the date of removal, but shall not participate in Carried Interest on future investment activities.

### Section 6.10 — Resignation of Manager

(a) The Manager may resign upon not less than one hundred eighty (180) days' prior written notice to all Limited Partners.

(b) Upon resignation, the Limited Partners holding a Supermajority Interest shall appoint a successor Manager within ninety (90) days.

(c) If no successor Manager is appointed within ninety (90) days of the Manager's notice of resignation, the Company shall be dissolved in accordance with Article XIII.

(d) Upon voluntary resignation (as opposed to removal for Cause), the Manager shall retain its Carried Interest and shall be entitled to receive any accrued but unpaid fees through the date of resignation.

## ARTICLE VII: Rights and Obligations of Members

### Section 7.1 — Limited Liability

(a) No Limited Partner shall be personally liable for any debts, obligations, or liabilities of the Company solely by reason of being a Limited Partner. The liability of each Limited Partner shall be limited to the amount of its Capital Contribution, its share of undistributed Company profits (if any), and its obligation to fund capital calls (if any), to the fullest extent permitted by the TBOC and applicable law.

(b) The Manager shall not be personally liable for the debts, obligations, or liabilities of the Company solely by reason of being the Manager, except to the extent that the Manager has personally guaranteed such debts, obligations, or liabilities, or to the extent that liability arises from the Manager's fraud, willful misconduct, or gross negligence.

### Section 7.2 — Voting Rights

(a) Each Class A Unit shall be entitled to one (1) vote on any matter submitted to a vote of the Limited Partners.

(b) Matters requiring the approval of the Limited Partners are limited to those expressly set forth in this Agreement, including the matters described in Section 6.3.

(c) A "Majority Interest" means Limited Partners holding more than fifty percent (50%) of the outstanding Class A Units.

(d) A "Supermajority Interest" means Limited Partners holding at least sixty-six and two-thirds percent (66.67%) of the outstanding Class A Units.

(e) Votes may be cast in person, by proxy, or by written consent. The Manager may conduct votes by electronic means, including email, online voting platforms, or digital signature services.

(f) Any action that may be taken at a meeting of the Members may be taken without a meeting if a written consent setting forth the action taken is signed by Members holding the requisite number of Units for such action.

### Section 7.3 — Meetings

(a) **Annual Meeting.** The Manager shall hold an annual meeting of the Members, which may be conducted in person, by videoconference, or by hybrid format, at such time and place as the Manager may designate. The annual meeting shall include a review of the Company's financial performance, development progress, and forward-looking business plan.

(b) **Special Meetings.** Special meetings of the Members may be called by: (i) the Manager; or (ii) Limited Partners holding at least twenty-five percent (25%) of the outstanding Class A Units, by written request to the Manager specifying the purpose(s) of the meeting.

(c) **Notice.** Written notice of any meeting shall be given to all Members at least fifteen (15) days prior to the meeting date. The notice shall specify the date, time, location (or videoconference information), and purpose(s) of the meeting.

(d) **Quorum.** Members holding a Majority Interest, present in person, by proxy, or by electronic participation, shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business at any meeting. If a quorum is not present, the meeting may be adjourned to a later date.

(e) **Minutes.** The Manager shall cause minutes of all meetings to be prepared and maintained as part of the Company's books and records.

### Section 7.4 — Information Rights

Each Limited Partner shall be entitled to receive the following information and reports from the Company:

| Report | Content | Timing |
|---|---|---|
| **Quarterly Financial Report** | Unaudited balance sheet, income statement, cash flow statement; development progress update; sales and leasing activity summary; distribution calculation and notice; management commentary and outlook | Within 45 days of each calendar quarter-end |
| **Annual Financial Report** | Audited financial statements prepared by independent CPA firm (balance sheet, income statement, statement of cash flows, notes to financial statements); management discussion and analysis; development progress and milestones; year-over-year comparison | Within 90 days of each fiscal year-end |
| **Annual Schedule K-1** | Each Member's allocable share of Company income, gain, loss, deduction, and credit for the fiscal year | On or before March 15 of the following year |
| **Distribution Notice** | Detailed calculation of distribution amount, waterfall tier, and per-unit distribution | Concurrent with each distribution |
| **Capital Account Statement** | Each Member's Capital Account balance, including contributions, distributions, allocations, and running balance | Annually (included with annual report) |
| **Material Event Notice** | Notification of material events including property acquisition, construction milestones, financing events, material litigation, and regulatory changes | Within 15 business days of the event |
| **Investor Portal Access** | Real-time online access to all reports, documents, tax forms, account information, and communications | Continuously available (24/7) |

### Section 7.5 — Inspection Rights

Each Limited Partner shall have the right to inspect and copy the books and records of the Company at reasonable times during normal business hours, upon fifteen (15) days' prior written notice to the Manager, at the Limited Partner's own expense. The Manager may impose reasonable conditions on such inspection, including requirements that the inspection be conducted at the Company's principal office, that the inspecting Member be accompanied by a representative of the Manager, and that proprietary or competitively sensitive information be treated as confidential.

### Section 7.6 — Confidentiality Obligations

Each Member shall maintain the confidentiality of all non-public information regarding the Company, the Project, the financial affairs of the Company, and the identity and affairs of the other Members, subject to the exceptions and provisions set forth in the Subscription Agreement (Document 04, Section 10).

### Section 7.7 — Competing Activities

(a) **Manager.** The Manager and its affiliates may engage in other business activities, including other real estate development projects that may compete with the Company for tenants, buyers, contractors, financing, or management attention. The doctrine of corporate opportunity shall not apply to the Manager or its affiliates. However, the Manager shall not divert to itself or its affiliates any specific business opportunity that was presented to the Manager in its capacity as Manager of the Company.

(b) **Limited Partners.** Limited Partners may engage in any business activities whatsoever, without restriction, including activities that may compete with the Company. No Limited Partner shall have any obligation to present business opportunities to the Company.

## ARTICLE VIII: Distributions

### Section 8.1 — Available Cash

"Available Cash" means, for any period, the excess (if any) of: (a) all cash receipts of the Company from all sources during such period (including, without limitation, sales proceeds, rental income, commercial lease revenue, retreat and event revenue, financing proceeds, capital contribution proceeds, interest and investment income, and any other cash receipts); over (b) all cash disbursements of the Company during such period (including, without limitation, operating expenses, debt service (principal and interest), capital expenditures, management fees, acquisition fees, construction management fees, disposition fees, taxes, insurance, and additions to reserves established by the Manager in its reasonable discretion).

### Section 8.2 — Distribution Waterfall

Available Cash from operations and capital events (sales, refinancings, dissolution proceeds) shall be distributed to the Members in the following order of priority:

**Tier 1 — Return of Capital**

First, one hundred percent (100%) to the Limited Partners, pro rata in accordance with their respective Percentage Interests, until each Limited Partner has received cumulative distributions equal to the aggregate of its Capital Contributions (the "Return of Capital").

**Tier 2 — Preferred Return**

Second, one hundred percent (100%) to the Limited Partners, pro rata in accordance with their respective Percentage Interests, until each Limited Partner has received cumulative distributions (in excess of the Return of Capital) equal to a cumulative annual return of eight percent (8%) on its unreturned Capital Contributions, compounded annually from the date of each Capital Contribution through the date of each distribution (the "Preferred Return").

For purposes of this Section, "unreturned Capital Contributions" means, at any point in time, the excess (if any) of a Limited Partner's aggregate Capital Contributions over the aggregate distributions previously received by such Limited Partner under Tier 1.

The Preferred Return is cumulative. Any accrued but unpaid Preferred Return from any period shall carry forward and compound annually until fully paid.

**Tier 3 — GP Catch-Up**

Third, eighty percent (80%) to the Limited Partners (pro rata) and twenty percent (20%) to the General Partner, until the General Partner has received cumulative distributions under this Tier 3 equal to twenty percent (20%) of the aggregate amount distributed under Tiers 2 and 3 combined.

**Tier 4 — Performance Split I (Above 15% IRR)**

Fourth, after the Limited Partners have achieved an internal rate of return of fifteen percent (15%) on their Capital Contributions (calculated on a blended, whole-fund basis using the XIRR methodology), seventy percent (70%) to the Limited Partners (pro rata) and thirty percent (30%) to the General Partner.

**Tier 5 — Performance Split II (Above 25% IRR)**

Fifth, after the Limited Partners have achieved an internal rate of return of twenty-five percent (25%) on their Capital Contributions, sixty percent (60%) to the Limited Partners (pro rata) and forty percent (40%) to the General Partner.

**Tier 6 — Performance Split III (Above 35% IRR)**

Sixth, after the Limited Partners have achieved an internal rate of return of thirty-five percent (35%) on their Capital Contributions, fifty percent (50%) to the Limited Partners (pro rata) and fifty percent (50%) to the General Partner.

### Section 8.3 — Waterfall Summary

| 1 | Return of Capital (1.0x) | 100% | 0% |
| 2 | Preferred Return (8% cumulative, compounding) | 100% | 0% |
| 3 | GP Catch-Up | 80% | 20% |
| 4 | Above 15% LP IRR | 70% | 30% |

### Section 8.4 — Timing of Distributions

(a) The Manager shall endeavor to make distributions of Available Cash on a quarterly basis, within thirty (30) days following the end of each calendar quarter.

(b) The Manager may also make special distributions from time to time in connection with capital events (property sales, refinancings, or other material liquidity events).

(c) Notwithstanding the foregoing, the timing and amount of all distributions are in the sole discretion of the Manager, subject to the maintenance of adequate reserves for operations, debt service, capital expenditures, contingencies, and compliance with any loan covenants or other contractual obligations. THERE IS NO OBLIGATION TO MAKE DISTRIBUTIONS ON ANY PARTICULAR SCHEDULE OR AT ALL.

(d) The Manager anticipates that the first quarterly distributions will commence approximately eighteen to twenty-four (18-24) months after the initial closing of the offering (estimated Q1 2028), subject to the Project generating sufficient Available Cash.

### Section 8.5 — Tax Distributions

(a) Notwithstanding Section 8.4, the Manager shall use commercially reasonable efforts to distribute to each Member, prior to the due date for quarterly estimated tax payments (April 15, June 15, September 15, and January 15), an amount reasonably estimated to be sufficient to cover such Member's federal and state income tax liability attributable to such Member's allocable share of Company taxable income for the relevant quarter (a "Tax Distribution"). Tax Distributions shall be calculated using the highest applicable combined federal and state individual marginal tax rate.

(b) Tax Distributions shall be treated as advances against, and shall reduce, the Member's entitlement to distributions under the waterfall set forth in Section 8.2.

(c) If a Member has received Tax Distributions in excess of the amount it would have received under the waterfall for the applicable period, such excess shall be deducted from future distributions otherwise payable to such Member.

### Section 8.6 — Withholding

The Company may withhold from any distribution such amounts as are required to be withheld under applicable federal, state, or local tax laws. Amounts withheld shall be treated as amounts distributed to the applicable Member for all purposes under this Agreement.

### Section 8.7 — Distributions in Kind

The Manager may, in its sole discretion, make distributions in kind (i.e., distributions of Company property other than cash). In such event: (a) the fair market value of the distributed property shall be determined by the Manager in good faith (or, if any Member objects, by an independent appraiser selected by the Manager); (b) the distribution shall be treated as a distribution of cash equal to such fair market value for purposes of the waterfall; and (c) the Members shall share in the distributed property in the same proportions as they would have shared in a cash distribution of equivalent value.

### Section 8.8 — Clawback Obligation

(a) **Final Waterfall Calculation.** Upon the final dissolution and liquidation of the Company (or upon the disposition of all Company assets, if earlier), the Manager shall perform a final calculation of the distribution waterfall based on the Company's total, overall performance from inception through termination (the "Final Waterfall Calculation").

(b) **Excess Distributions.** If the Final Waterfall Calculation reveals that the General Partner has received cumulative distributions in excess of the amounts that the General Partner would have been entitled to receive under the waterfall based on the Company's overall total performance, the General Partner shall return such excess to the Company for redistribution to the Limited Partners in accordance with their Percentage Interests (the "Clawback Amount").

(c) **Payment.** The Clawback Amount shall be paid by the General Partner within ninety (90) days of the Final Waterfall Calculation.

(d) **Limitation.** The General Partner's Clawback obligation shall not exceed the cumulative Carried Interest (Tiers 3, 4, 5, and 6 GP distributions) received by the General Partner, net of income taxes actually paid by the General Partner on such Carried Interest (computed at the maximum applicable tax rate).

(e) **Security.** The General Partner agrees to maintain adequate reserves or provide such other security as the Manager and the Advisory Committee (if any) may agree to ensure the General Partner's ability to satisfy the Clawback obligation.

### Section 8.9 — No Demand Rights

Except as expressly provided in this Article VIII, no Member shall have the right to demand or compel distributions from the Company. The Manager's discretion regarding the timing and amount of distributions is absolute, subject only to the waterfall priorities and the Clawback obligation.

## ARTICLE IX: Allocations of Profits and Losses

### Section 9.1 — General Allocations

(a) **Profits.** Company profits (and each item of income and gain comprising such profits) for each fiscal year shall be allocated among the Members in a manner that, to the extent possible, causes each Member's Capital Account balance, immediately after such allocation, to equal the amount that such Member would receive if all Company assets were sold for their book values, all Company liabilities were satisfied according to their terms, and the remaining proceeds were distributed in accordance with the waterfall set forth in Section 8.2.

(b) **Losses.** Company losses (and each item of deduction and loss comprising such losses) for each fiscal year shall be allocated among the Members in a manner that, to the extent possible, causes each Member's Capital Account balance, immediately after such allocation, to equal the amount that such Member would receive if all Company assets were sold for their book values, all Company liabilities were satisfied, and the remaining proceeds were distributed in accordance with the waterfall set forth in Section 8.2.

(c) **Limitation on Loss Allocations.** No allocation of loss or deduction shall be made to any Member to the extent that such allocation would cause or increase a deficit in such Member's Capital Account in excess of the amount such Member is obligated to restore pursuant to this Agreement or Treasury Regulation Section 1.704-1(b)(2)(ii)(d). Any loss or deduction that cannot be allocated to a Member because of this limitation shall be allocated to the other Members in accordance with their respective Percentage Interests.

### Section 9.2 — Regulatory Allocations

Notwithstanding the general allocations in Section 9.1, the following special allocations shall be made in the order and priority indicated, and shall be applied to determine taxable income or loss:

(a) **Minimum Gain Chargeback.** If there is a net decrease in Company Minimum Gain (as defined in Treasury Regulation Sections 1.704-2(b)(2) and 1.704-2(d)) during any fiscal year, each Member shall be specially allocated items of Company income and gain for such fiscal year (and, if necessary, subsequent fiscal years) in proportion to, and to the extent of, such Member's share of the net decrease in Company Minimum Gain, as determined under Treasury Regulation Section 1.704-2(g). This Section 9.2(a) is intended to comply with the minimum gain chargeback requirement of Treasury Regulation Section 1.704-2(f) and shall be interpreted consistently therewith.

(b) **Member Nonrecourse Debt Minimum Gain Chargeback.** If there is a net decrease in Member Nonrecourse Debt Minimum Gain (as defined in Treasury Regulation Section 1.704-2(i)(3)) during any fiscal year attributable to a Member Nonrecourse Debt, each Member who has a share of such Member Nonrecourse Debt Minimum Gain shall be specially allocated items of Company income and gain for such year in proportion to, and to the extent of, such Member's share of the net decrease in Member Nonrecourse Debt Minimum Gain, as determined under Treasury Regulation Section 1.704-2(i)(4). This Section 9.2(b) is intended to comply with the partner nonrecourse debt minimum gain chargeback requirement of Treasury Regulation Section 1.704-2(i)(4) and shall be interpreted consistently therewith.

(c) **Qualified Income Offset.** If any Member unexpectedly receives an adjustment, allocation, or distribution described in Treasury Regulation Section 1.704-1(b)(2)(ii)(d)(4), (5), or (6) that causes or increases a deficit balance in such Member's Capital Account in excess of the amount such Member is obligated to restore, items of Company income and gain shall be specially allocated to such Member in an amount and manner sufficient to eliminate such excess deficit as quickly as possible. This Section 9.2(c) is intended to constitute a "qualified income offset" within the meaning of Treasury Regulation Section 1.704-1(b)(2)(ii)(d)(3) and shall be interpreted consistently therewith.

(d) **Nonrecourse Deductions.** Nonrecourse Deductions (as defined in Treasury Regulation Section 1.704-2(b)(1)) for any fiscal year shall be allocated among the Members in proportion to their respective Percentage Interests.

(e) **Member Nonrecourse Deductions.** Any Member Nonrecourse Deductions (as defined in Treasury Regulation Section 1.704-2(i)(2)) for any fiscal year shall be allocated to the Member who bears the economic risk of loss with respect to the Member Nonrecourse Debt to which such deductions are attributable, as determined under Treasury Regulation Section 1.704-2(i)(1).

### Section 9.3 — Tax Allocations Under Section 704(c)

In accordance with Section 704(c) of the Code and Treasury Regulation Section 1.704-1(b)(4)(i), income, gain, loss, and deduction with respect to any property contributed to the Company shall, solely for tax purposes, be allocated among the Members so as to take account of any variation between the adjusted tax basis of such property to the Company for federal income tax purposes and the initial book value of such property at the time of contribution, using the "traditional method" described in Treasury Regulation Section 1.704-3(b), or such other reasonable method as the Manager may select consistent with Section 704(c) of the Code and the applicable Treasury Regulations.

### Section 9.4 — Allocation Among Transferors and Transferees

If any Member's interest is transferred during a fiscal year, the Company's income, gain, loss, deduction, and credit attributable to such interest for such fiscal year shall be allocated between the transferor and the transferee using the "interim closing of the books" method under Section 706(d) of the Code and the applicable Treasury Regulations. The Manager may, in its discretion, elect to use the "proration method" permitted under Treasury Regulation Section 1.706-1(c)(2) if the Manager determines that such method is more practical and does not materially distort allocations.

### Section 9.5 — Intent

The allocation provisions of this Article IX are intended to comply with Treasury Regulation Section 1.704-1(b) and Section 1.704-2 and shall be interpreted and applied in a manner consistent therewith. If the Manager determines that it is necessary to modify the allocations set forth herein to comply with such Regulations, the Manager may make such modifications, provided that such modifications are not likely to have a material adverse effect on the amounts distributable to any Member.

The Company shall be dissolved upon the first to occur of the following events:

(a) The affirmative written consent of Limited Partners holding a Supermajority Interest (66.67% of Class A Units) to dissolve the Company;

(b) The sale, exchange, or other disposition of all or substantially all of the Company's assets and the distribution of all net proceeds to the Members in accordance with Article VIII;

(d) The occurrence of any event that makes it unlawful for the Company to continue its business, unless the illegality is cured within ninety (90) days;

(e) The death, withdrawal, resignation, expulsion, bankruptcy, insolvency, or dissolution of the Manager, unless a successor Manager is appointed by Limited Partners holding a Supermajority Interest within ninety (90) days of such event; or

(f) Any other event specified by the TBOC as causing dissolution of a limited liability company, to the extent not superseded by the provisions of this Agreement.

The Company shall not be dissolved by the withdrawal, death, bankruptcy, insolvency, or dissolution of any Limited Partner, or by the Transfer of any Limited Partner's interest. Upon any such event, the Company shall continue and the withdrawing, deceased, bankrupt, insolvent, or dissolved Limited Partner (or its estate, successor, or transferee) shall be treated as an assignee under Section 10.6.

### Section 13.3 — Winding Up

(a) Upon dissolution, the Manager (or, if the Manager is unavailable or has been removed, a liquidating agent appointed by Limited Partners holding a Majority Interest) shall wind up the Company's affairs in an orderly and commercially reasonable manner.

(b) The winding up process shall include:

   (i) Collecting all accounts receivable, loans, and other amounts owed to the Company;

   (ii) Completing any construction in progress to the extent commercially reasonable and selling completed inventory;

   (iii) Liquidating remaining assets, including real property, at commercially reasonable prices (the Manager may distribute assets in kind if it determines that in-kind distribution is more advantageous to the Members than a forced sale);

   (iv) Paying or adequately providing for all debts, liabilities, obligations, and expenses of the Company (in order of priority required by the TBOC and applicable law);

   (v) Establishing such reserves as the Manager deems reasonably necessary for contingent, conditional, or unliquidated liabilities or obligations of the Company (such reserves may be held for a period the Manager deems appropriate and shall be distributed to the Members upon release); and

   (vi) Distributing the remaining assets (cash and/or property) to the Members in accordance with Section 13.4.

### Section 13.4 — Distribution Upon Liquidation

After payment (or adequate provision for payment) of all Company debts, liabilities, and obligations, and after establishment of any reserves deemed necessary by the Manager or liquidating agent, the remaining assets of the Company shall be distributed to the Members as follows:

(a) **First**, to the Members in accordance with the positive balances in their respective Capital Accounts, after taking into account all final allocations of income, gain, loss, deduction, and credit for the fiscal year (or partial fiscal year) during which liquidation occurs.

(b) For the avoidance of doubt, liquidating distributions shall follow the waterfall priorities established in Section 8.2, such that:

   (i) Limited Partners receive their unreturned Capital Contributions (Tier 1);

   (ii) Limited Partners receive their accrued Preferred Return (Tier 2);

   (iii) The GP Catch-Up is satisfied (Tier 3);

   (iv) Any remaining amounts are distributed in accordance with the applicable Performance Split tiers (Tiers 4, 5, and 6); and

   (v) The Clawback obligation (Section 8.8) is applied to ensure whole-fund fairness.

### Section 13.5 — Final Accounting and Report

Within one hundred twenty (120) days following the final distribution, the Manager (or liquidating agent) shall prepare and deliver to all Members a final accounting of the Company, including:

(a) A final balance sheet and income statement;

(b) A final determination of each Member's Capital Account balance;

(c) A reconciliation of all contributions, allocations, and distributions;

(d) A schedule of the Final Waterfall Calculation; and

(e) Any Clawback amounts due and paid (or payable).

### Section 13.6 — Certificate of Termination
