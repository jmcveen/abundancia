## 1. Valuation Overview

### 1.1 Purpose of Valuation

This report presents a comprehensive valuation analysis of Abundancia Austin, a 376-acre regenerative master-planned community in Cedar Creek, Bastrop County, Texas. The valuation is conducted in connection with a $12.5M capital raise under Regulation D, Rule 506(c), and is intended to provide prospective investors with a transparent, multi-methodology assessment of project value at current stage.

| Attribute | Detail |

| **Zoning** | No county-level zoning (Bastrop County) |
| **Development Program** | 680 residential units, 200 finished lots, 60 rental units, commercial, retreat |
| **Projected 10-Year Revenue** | $435M (base case) |
| **Projected 10-Year EBITDA** | $104M (base case) |
| **Capital Raise** | $12.5M |
| **Entity Structure** | TX Series LLC, LP/GP, Reg D 506(c) |

### 1.3 Valuation Date

All analyses are conducted as of **March 1, 2026**, reflecting current market conditions, comparable transactions through Q4 2025, and forward projections based on management's development plan.

### 1.4 Scope of Work

Five independent valuation methodologies have been applied to triangulate a defensible value range:

1. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Analysis
2. Comparable Transaction Method
3. Replacement Cost Method
4. Income Capitalization Approach
5. Development Profit Method

## 2. Methodology Summary

| Method | Approach | Strengths | Best For | Weight |
|--------|----------|-----------|----------|--------|
| **DCF Analysis** | Projects 10 years of cash flows, discounts to present value at risk-adjusted rate | Captures full development timeline and exit value | Development-stage projects with multi-year cash flow profiles | 35% |
| **Comparable Transactions** | Benchmarks against recent land sales in Austin/Bastrop corridors | Market-validated pricing per acre | Establishing current land basis and market support | 20% |
| **Replacement Cost** | Calculates total cost to replicate land position + planned infrastructure + vertical construction | Establishes floor value and cost basis | Determining minimum defensible value | 15% |
| **Income Capitalization** | Capitalizes stabilized NOI from income-producing components at market cap rates | Standard institutional methodology for income assets | Rental, commercial, and retreat revenue components | 15% |
| **Development Profit** | Total projected revenue less total projected costs less required developer return | Tests whether the project "pencils" at the proposed valuation | Validating residual land/entity value | 15% |

## 8. Reconciliation of Methods

### 8.1 Summary of Indicated Values

| Valuation Method | Indicated Value | Stage Adjustment | Current-Stage Value | Weight |
|-----------------|----------------|-----------------|-------------------|--------|
| **DCF Analysis** | $120.3M | — | $120.3M | 35% |

### 8.2 Reconciliation Discussion

**DCF Analysis (35% weight):** The DCF method receives the highest weighting as it most accurately captures the multi-phase development timeline, the diversity of revenue streams, and the time value of money over the full 10-year horizon. The $120.3M indicated value reflects the NPV of projected cash flows plus terminal value, which is the most relevant framework for a development-stage real estate investment.

**Comparable Transactions (20% weight):** The $56.4M comparable transaction value reflects the current "as-is" value of the land position with a planning/entitlement premium. This establishes a defensible floor value supported by recent market transactions. The 20% weighting reflects the fact that raw land comparables do not capture the value of the development plan, team, and entitlement progress — but they provide essential market grounding.

**Replacement Cost (15% weight):** At full buildout, the replacement cost value of $285.4M significantly exceeds other methodologies, as expected for a project at development stage. The 40% stage-of-completion factor reflects the current entitlement progress, engineering completion, and team assembly. This method confirms that the project's "cost to recreate" significantly exceeds the proposed investment basis.

**Income Capitalization (15% weight):** The $113.1M income-based value combines direct capitalization of income-producing assets with the NPV of for-sale proceeds. This method validates the project's income-generating capacity once stabilized and provides comfort to investors focused on cash yield.

**Development Profit (15% weight):** The $95.2M residual value confirms that the project generates sufficient margin above costs and required returns to justify the proposed valuation. This method serves as a cross-check rather than a primary indicator.
