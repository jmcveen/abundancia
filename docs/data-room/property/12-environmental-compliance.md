## 2. Houston Toad Habitat

### 2.1 Species Profile

The Houston toad (*Bufo houstonensis*) is one of the most endangered amphibians in North America. Listed as endangered under the ESA in 1970, the species has experienced dramatic population declines due to habitat loss, drought, and the 2011 Bastrop Complex Fire.

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Scientific Name** | *Bufo houstonensis* (syn. *Anaxyrus houstonensis*) |
| **ESA Listing** | Endangered (35 FR 16047, October 13, 1970) |
| **Critical Habitat** | Designated in Bastrop County (71 FR 18740, April 12, 2006) |
| **Recovery Plan** | Houston Toad Recovery Plan (USFWS, 1984; revised) |
| **Remaining Populations** | Bastrop County is the primary remaining stronghold |
| **Population Trend** | Declining — exacerbated by 2011 fire and drought cycles |
| **Primary Threats** | Habitat loss, fragmentation, drought, fire, disease |

### 2.2 Habitat Requirements

| Habitat Element | Requirement | On-Site Availability |
|----------------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Sandy soils** | Deep sandy or sandy loam for burrowing | Present — Patilo, Axtell, Silstid series |
| **Breeding ponds** | Ephemeral / semi-permanent standing water (Jan-Jun) | Present — 7 retention ponds |
| **Upland habitat** | Pine/oak woodland within 1 km of breeding ponds | Present — 205+ acres woodland |
| **Canopy cover** | Open to moderate canopy (30-70%) | Present — post-fire recovery stage |
| **Understory** | Leaf litter, ground cover for moisture retention | Present — recovering |
| **Connectivity** | Unbroken corridors between breeding and upland sites | Present — planned conservation corridors |

### 2.3 On-Site Habitat Assessment

| Habitat Type | Acreage | Houston Toad Suitability | Conservation Action |
|-------------|---------|-------------------------|-------------------|
| Sandy upland woodland | 85 | High — primary burrowing habitat | Full conservation easement |
| Post oak savannah | 72 | Moderate — secondary upland habitat | Conservation easement |
| Retention ponds + margins | 34 | High — breeding habitat | Full conservation, enhanced buffers |
| Riparian corridors | 28 | Moderate — movement corridors | Conservation buffer (100 ft min) |
| Grassland / prairie | 95 | Low-Moderate — marginal habitat | Partial conservation, partial agriculture |
| Disturbed / cleared | 30 | Low — degraded | Development zone (with mitigation) |

### 2.4 Impact of the 2011 Bastrop Complex Fire

The 2011 Bastrop Complex Fire was the most destructive wildfire in Texas history:

| Parameter | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| **Total area burned** | 34,000 acres |
| **Homes destroyed** | 1,673 |
| **Houston toad habitat burned** | Estimated 60-70% of Bastrop County habitat |
| **Population impact** | Catastrophic — multiple breeding populations eliminated |
| **Recovery timeline** | 15+ years for habitat, decades for toad population |
| **Current status (2026)** | Habitat recovering; toad populations remain critically low |

The fire created both a conservation imperative and a development opportunity: properly designed conservation communities can actively restore habitat while the surrounding landscape recovers.

## 3. Lost Pines Habitat Conservation Plan (LPHCP)

### 3.1 LPHCP Overview

The Lost Pines Habitat Conservation Plan is the primary regulatory framework governing development in Houston toad habitat within Bastrop County. Administered by Bastrop County under an Incidental Take Permit (ITP) from the USFWS, the LPHCP allows covered development activities to proceed provided specific conservation measures are met.

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Permit Holder** | Bastrop County |
| **Permit Type** | ESA Section 10(a)(1)(B) Incidental Take Permit |
| **Species Covered** | Houston toad (*Bufo houstonensis*) |
| **Geographic Scope** | Bastrop County (designated areas within HCP boundary) |
| **Permit Duration** | 30 years |
| **Mitigation Framework** | Conservation ratios, habitat preservation, fees |
| **Administering Entity** | Bastrop County / Environmental Affairs Office |

### 3.2 LPHCP Conservation Tiers

The LPHCP classifies land into tiers based on Houston toad habitat quality:

| Tier | Description | Development Allowed | Conservation Ratio | Mitigation Fee |
|------|-------------|--------------------|--------------------|---------------|
| **Tier 1** | Primary breeding habitat / critical | Very limited | 3:1 (3 acres conserved per 1 developed) | Highest |
| **Tier 2** | Secondary habitat / moderate quality | Moderate with mitigation | 2:1 | Moderate |
| **Tier 3** | Marginal habitat / previously disturbed | Standard with mitigation | 1:1 | Standard |
| **Tier 4** | Non-habitat / already developed | Standard permitting | None required | None |

### 7.3 Prescribed Fire Management

Prescribed fire is an essential tool for maintaining the Lost Pines ecosystem, which evolved under a natural fire regime. Post-2011, managed fire return promotes:

- Pine seedling establishment (fire-adapted species)
- Understory diversity and Houston toad foraging habitat
- Invasive species suppression
- Fuel load reduction (wildfire risk mitigation)

| Parameter | Specification |
|-----------|--------------|
| Fire return interval | 3-5 years (rotational by management unit) |
| Burn season | December - February (outside toad breeding season) |
| Unit size | 10-25 acres per burn |
| Permitting | Bastrop County burn permit + TPWD coordination |
| Insurance | Required — prescribed burn liability coverage |
| Personnel | Certified prescribed burn manager + crew |
