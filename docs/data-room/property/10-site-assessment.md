## 5. Vegetation Assessment

### 5.1 Lost Pines Ecosystem

The property lies within the Lost Pines ecosystem, an isolated stand of loblolly pine (*Pinus taeda*) located approximately 100 miles west of the main East Texas pine belt. This ecologically significant forest exists due to the unique sandy soil conditions that distinguish this region from the surrounding Blackland Prairie.

The 2011 Bastrop Complex Fire — the most destructive wildfire in Texas history — burned 34,000 acres and destroyed 1,673 homes in this region. The fire fundamentally altered the Lost Pines landscape, and the property reflects the ongoing ecological recovery process.
