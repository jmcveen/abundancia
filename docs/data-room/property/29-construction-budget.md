## 1. Executive Summary

Abundancia Austin is a $12,500,000 regenerative luxury community development on 120 acres in the Lost Pines region of Bastrop County, Texas, approximately 30 minutes east of downtown Austin. The project delivers 50 hempcrete homesites across two phases, supported by shared community amenities, off-grid energy and water infrastructure, and regenerative land systems.

### 1.3 Hempcrete Construction Strategy

Hempcrete (hemp-lime composite) is the cornerstone building material for all Abundancia structures. This choice drives both the premium positioning and the regenerative mission of the project.

| Hempcrete Advantage | Impact on Budget |
|---------------------|-----------------|
| Carbon-negative lifecycle | Qualifies for 45Q-adjacent incentives; premium pricing justified |
| Superior thermal mass (R-value 2.4/inch at 12" wall) | Reduces HVAC sizing by 40-50%, lowering mechanical costs |
| Breathable wall assembly | Eliminates vapor barrier costs; reduces mold remediation risk |
| Fire resistance (Class A/1-hour rating) | Lower insurance premiums (est. 15-20% reduction) |
| Acoustic insulation | Reduces need for supplemental soundproofing |
| Material cost premium | +$18-25/sf vs. conventional framing (offset by reduced mechanical) |

**Net premium after mechanical offsets: approximately 18-22% above conventional wood-frame construction in Central Texas.**
