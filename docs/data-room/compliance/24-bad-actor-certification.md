## 1. Purpose and Legal Basis

### 1.1 Overview

This Bad Actor Certification and Disqualification Verification memorandum has been prepared in connection with the offering of membership interests ("Interests") in Abundancia Austin Holdings, LLC, a Texas Series Limited Liability Company (the "Company"), pursuant to Rule 506(c) of Regulation D under the Securities Act of 1933, as amended (the "Securities Act").

### 1.2 Rule 506(d) Requirements

Rule 506(d) of Regulation D, adopted by the Securities and Exchange Commission ("SEC") on July 10, 2013, disqualifies an issuer from relying on the Rule 506 exemption if the issuer or any other "covered person" has experienced a "disqualifying event" as enumerated in Rule 506(d)(1)(i) through (viii).

The rule applies to all offerings conducted under both Rule 506(b) and Rule 506(c). Compliance is mandatory; failure to verify the absence of disqualifying events renders the Rule 506 exemption unavailable, potentially exposing the offering to rescission liability and SEC enforcement action.

### 1.3 Applicability to This Offering

The Company is conducting a capital raise of up to **$12,500,000** in membership interests under Rule 506(c) of Regulation D. The offering is limited exclusively to accredited investors as defined in Rule 501(a) of Regulation D, with general solicitation permitted under 506(c) subject to reasonable steps to verify accredited investor status.

As a condition to reliance on Rule 506(c), the Company has conducted a thorough investigation to determine whether any covered person associated with this offering is subject to any disqualifying event described in Rule 506(d)(1).

### 1.4 Relevant Time Period

Disqualifying events are subject to the following look-back periods:

| Event Category | Look-Back Period |
|---|---|
| Criminal convictions | 10 years (5 years for issuers) |
| Court injunctions and restraining orders | 5 years |
| Final orders from state regulators | 10 years |
| SEC disciplinary orders | At time of sale |
| SEC cease-and-desist orders | At time of sale |
| SEC stop orders and suspension orders | At time of sale |
| SRO suspensions or expulsions | At time of sale |
| USPS false representation orders | 5 years |

Events occurring prior to September 23, 2013 are subject to the disclosure provisions of Rule 506(e) rather than the disqualification provisions of Rule 506(d).

## 3. Disqualifying Events

### 3.1 Overview

Rule 506(d)(1) identifies eight categories of disqualifying events. A covered person who has experienced any of the following events within the applicable look-back period is disqualified from participating in a Rule 506 offering:

### 3.2 Category (i) — Criminal Convictions

Any conviction, within ten years before such sale (or five years, in the case of issuers, their predecessors and affiliated issuers), of any felony or misdemeanor:

- In connection with the purchase or sale of any security
- Involving the making of any false filing with the SEC
- Arising out of the conduct of the business of an underwriter, broker, dealer, municipal securities dealer, investment adviser, or paid solicitor of purchasers of securities

### 3.3 Category (ii) — Court Injunctions and Restraining Orders

Any court injunction or restraining order issued within five years before such sale, in connection with:

- The purchase or sale of any security
- The making of any false filing with the SEC
- The conduct of the business of an underwriter, broker, dealer, municipal securities dealer, investment adviser, or paid solicitor of purchasers of securities

### 3.4 Category (iii) — State Regulatory Final Orders

Being subject to a final order of a state securities commission (or an agency or officer of a state performing like functions), a state authority that supervises or examines banks, savings associations, or credit unions, a state insurance commission (or an agency or officer of a state performing like functions), an appropriate federal banking agency, or the National Credit Union Administration, that:

- At the time of such sale, bars the person from association with an entity regulated by such commission, authority, agency, or officer, or from engaging in the business of securities, insurance, or banking, or from engaging in savings association or credit union activities; **or**
- Constitutes a final order based on a violation of any law or regulation that prohibits fraudulent, manipulative, or deceptive conduct and was issued within ten years before such sale

### 3.5 Category (iv) — SEC Disciplinary Orders

Being subject to an order of the SEC entered pursuant to:

- Section 15(b) or 15B(c) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 ("Exchange Act")
- Section 203(e) or 203(f) of the Investment Advisers Act of 1940 ("Advisers Act")

that, at the time of such sale:

- Suspends or revokes such person's registration as a broker, dealer, municipal securities dealer, or investment adviser
- Places limitations on the activities, functions, or operations of such person
- Bars such person from being associated with any entity or from participating in the offering of any penny stock

### 3.6 Category (v) — SEC Cease-and-Desist Orders

Being subject to any order of the SEC entered within five years before such sale that, at the time of such sale, orders the person to cease and desist from committing or causing a violation or future violation of:

- Any scienter-based anti-fraud provision of the federal securities laws, including but not limited to:
  - Section 17(a)(1) of the Securities Act
  - Section 10(b) of the Exchange Act and Rule 10b-5 thereunder
  - Section 15(c)(1) of the Exchange Act
  - Section 206(1) of the Advisers Act, or any other rule or regulation thereunder
- Section 5 of the Securities Act

### 3.7 Category (vi) — SRO Suspension or Expulsion

Being suspended or expelled from membership in, or suspended or barred from association with a member of, a registered national securities exchange or a registered national securities association for any act or omission to act constituting conduct inconsistent with just and equitable principles of trade.

### 3.8 Category (vii) — SEC Registration Stop Orders

Having filed (as a registrant or issuer), or being or having been named as an underwriter in, any registration statement or Regulation A offering statement filed with the SEC that, within five years before such sale, was the subject of a refusal order, stop order, or order suspending the Regulation A exemption, or is, at the time of such sale, the subject of an investigation or proceeding to determine whether a stop order or suspension order should be issued.

### 3.9 Category (viii) — United States Postal Service False Representation Orders

Being subject to a United States Postal Service false representation order entered within five years before such sale, or being, at the time of such sale, subject to a temporary restraining order or preliminary injunction with respect to conduct alleged by the United States Postal Service to constitute a scheme or device for obtaining money or property through the mail by means of false representations.

## 6. Annual Update Procedures

### 6.1 Ongoing Monitoring Obligations

The Company will maintain its bad actor verification on an ongoing basis throughout the term of the offering by implementing the following procedures:

### 6.2 Annual Recertification

- Each covered person shall execute an annual certification questionnaire confirming the continued absence of disqualifying events
- Annual recertification shall be completed no later than **January 31** of each calendar year
- The Compliance Officer shall maintain records of all certifications

### 6.3 Event-Driven Updates

- All covered persons are required to notify the Company immediately (and in no event later than 5 business days) upon the occurrence of any disqualifying event or any event that may reasonably be expected to result in a disqualifying event
- This notification obligation is a condition of continued association with the offering

### 6.4 New Covered Persons

- Prior to the engagement of any new covered person (including placement agents, compensated solicitors, or new officers/directors), the Company shall:
  - Conduct full disqualification verification using the methodology described in Section 5
  - Obtain a written certification from the prospective covered person
  - Document the results and update this memorandum

### 6.5 Record Retention

- All verification records, search results, and certifications shall be maintained for a minimum period of **five (5) years** following the termination of the offering
- Records shall be made available to the SEC upon request

## 7. Pre-Closing Certification Requirement

### 7.1 Closing Condition

As a condition to each closing of the sale of Interests, the Manager of the Company shall certify that:

1. A reasonable investigation has been conducted to determine whether any covered person is subject to a disqualifying event under Rule 506(d)(1)
2. No covered person is subject to any disqualifying event as of the date of the closing
3. No event has occurred since the date of the most recent verification that would constitute or give rise to a disqualifying event
4. All certifications from covered persons are current and have not been revoked or amended

### 7.2 Form of Certification

The Manager's certification shall be in substantially the following form:

> *The undersigned, in his/her capacity as [title] of Abundancia Austin GP, LLC, the Manager of Abundancia Austin Holdings, LLC (the "Company"), hereby certifies that as of [date], after reasonable investigation: (a) no covered person (as defined in Rule 506(d) of Regulation D) associated with the offering of membership interests in the Company is subject to any disqualifying event described in Rule 506(d)(1)(i) through (viii); and (b) the Company has complied with the verification procedures described in the Company's Bad Actor Certification and Disqualification Verification memorandum dated March 2026.*

### 7.3 Remediation

In the event that a disqualifying event is discovered with respect to any covered person:

1. The Company shall immediately cease sales of Interests pending evaluation
2. Legal counsel shall evaluate whether the disqualification may be waived pursuant to Rule 506(d)(2) (by application to the SEC) or whether the event falls within the pre-existing event exception under Rule 506(e)
3. If the event cannot be waived or excepted, the affected covered person shall be removed from participation in the offering prior to any further sales
4. Full disclosure of the event shall be provided to prospective investors as required by Rule 506(e)
