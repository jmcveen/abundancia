## 1. Legal Framework

### 1.1 Applicable Laws and Regulations

Abundancia Austin Holdings, LLC (the "Company") has adopted these Anti-Money Laundering and Know Your Customer Procedures in recognition of the regulatory environment governing private securities offerings and real estate transactions. While the Company is not a financial institution subject to the full requirements of the Bank Secrecy Act ("BSA"), the Company has voluntarily implemented these procedures as a matter of best practice and investor protection.

The following laws, regulations, and guidance inform these procedures:

| Law / Regulation | Citation | Applicability |
|---|---|---|
| Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) | 31 U.S.C. §§ 5311-5332 | Framework for anti-money laundering requirements |
| USA PATRIOT Act | Pub. L. 107-56 (2001) | Expanded AML requirements, customer identification |
| OFAC Regulations | 31 C.F.R. Chapter V | Sanctions compliance, SDN screening |
| FinCEN Customer Due Diligence Rule | 31 C.F.R. § 1010.230 | Beneficial ownership identification |
| FinCEN Geographic Targeting Orders | 31 C.F.R. § 1010.370 | Real estate transaction reporting (select markets) |
| FinCEN Beneficial Ownership Reporting | 31 C.F.R. § 1010.380 (Corporate Transparency Act) | Entity beneficial ownership disclosure |
| SEC Regulation D | 17 C.F.R. §§ 230.501-506 | Investor verification requirements |
| Texas Securities Act | Tex. Rev. Civ. Stat. Ann. art. 581 | State securities law compliance |

### 1.2 FinCEN Geographic Targeting Orders (GTOs)

The Company acknowledges that FinCEN has issued Geographic Targeting Orders requiring certain reporting for all-cash real estate transactions in designated metropolitan areas. While Bastrop County, Texas is not currently within a designated GTO area, the Company will monitor GTO amendments and comply with any future orders that may apply to the project's geographic area.

### 1.3 Corporate Transparency Act (CTA)

The Company and Abundancia Austin GP, LLC will comply with the Corporate Transparency Act's beneficial ownership reporting requirements as applicable, including timely filing of Beneficial Ownership Information Reports with FinCEN.

## 2. Company Policy Statement

### 2.1 Policy Declaration

It is the policy of Abundancia Austin Holdings, LLC and Abundancia Austin GP, LLC to:

- Conduct appropriate due diligence on all prospective investors prior to acceptance of subscription funds
- Prohibit the acceptance of investments from any individual or entity that appears on the OFAC Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons List or any other applicable sanctions list
- Refuse to accept investments where there is reason to believe the source of funds involves money laundering, terrorist financing, or other illicit activity
- Maintain adequate records to demonstrate compliance with AML/KYC procedures
- Report suspicious activity to appropriate authorities as required by law
- Train all personnel involved in investor relations and fund administration on AML/KYC requirements

### 2.2 Zero Tolerance

The Company maintains a zero-tolerance policy for money laundering, terrorist financing, sanctions violations, and all forms of financial crime. Any violation of this policy by Company personnel is grounds for immediate termination and referral to law enforcement.

### 2.3 Commitment to Cooperation

The Company will cooperate fully with all lawful requests from regulatory authorities, law enforcement agencies, and financial intelligence units in connection with AML investigations.

## 3. Risk Assessment

The Company has conducted a risk assessment of its offering and investor base to determine appropriate AML/KYC measures:

| Risk Factor | Assessment | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Product Type | Medium | Private real estate securities offering; illiquid, long-term hold |

### 3.2 Investor Type Risk Classification

| Investor Type | Risk Level | Additional Measures Required |
|---|---|---|
| U.S. individual — known relationship | Low | Standard CIP |
| U.S. individual — no prior relationship | Low-Medium | Standard CIP + enhanced source of funds inquiry |
| U.S. trust or family office | Low-Medium | Standard CIP + beneficial ownership identification |
| U.S. corporation or LLC | Medium | Standard CIP + beneficial ownership + entity verification |

| Investment from high-risk jurisdiction | High | Enhanced Due Diligence; may be declined |

### 3.3 Red Flags

The following conditions are considered red flags that trigger Enhanced Due Diligence or potential rejection:

- Investor reluctance to provide identification documentation
- Inconsistent or implausible information on subscription documents
- Funds originating from a jurisdiction identified as high-risk by FATF
- Investment size inconsistent with known investor profile
- Request for unusual structuring of the investment
- Third-party funding without clear relationship or explanation
- Investor included on any sanctions, watch list, or adverse media screening
- Use of shell companies or complex structures without clear business purpose
- Cash or cash-equivalent payments (Company does not accept cash)
- Requests for rapid withdrawal or return of funds shortly after investment

## 5. Accredited Investor Verification

### 5.1 Requirement Under Rule 506(c)

As the offering is conducted under Rule 506(c) of Regulation D, which permits general solicitation, the Company is required to take **reasonable steps to verify** that each purchaser is an accredited investor as defined in Rule 501(a) of Regulation D. Self-certification alone is not sufficient under 506(c).

### 5.2 Verification Methods

The Company will accept one or more of the following verification methods as constituting reasonable steps:

**For Income-Based Qualification (Rule 501(a)(6)):**

| Method | Documentation Required |
|---|---|
| Tax return review | IRS Forms W-2, 1099, K-1, or federal income tax returns for the two most recent years, plus a written representation of reasonable expectation of meeting the income threshold in the current year |
| Third-party verification | Written confirmation from a registered broker-dealer, SEC-registered investment adviser, licensed CPA, or licensed attorney that such person has taken reasonable steps to verify accredited status within the prior three months |

**For Net Worth-Based Qualification (Rule 501(a)(5)):**

| Method | Documentation Required |
|---|---|
| Asset and liability review | Bank statements, brokerage statements, certificates of deposit, tax assessments, and appraisal reports (assets); credit report dated within 90 days (liabilities) |
| Third-party verification | Written confirmation from a registered broker-dealer, SEC-registered investment adviser, licensed CPA, or licensed attorney |

**For Entity-Based Qualification:**

| Entity Type | Verification Method |
|---|---|
| Entity with $5M+ assets (Rule 501(a)(3)) | Audited or reviewed financial statements, or other reliable documentation of assets |
| Entity with all equity owners accredited | Individual verification of each equity owner |
| Registered broker-dealer | FINRA registration verification |
| Bank, insurance company, registered investment company | Regulatory registration verification |
| Family office with $5M+ AUM | AUM documentation and family client confirmation |

### 5.3 Third-Party Verification Letter

The Company may accept a written verification letter from any of the following:

- A registered broker-dealer (FINRA member)
- An investment adviser registered with the SEC
- A licensed certified public accountant (CPA)
- A licensed attorney in good standing

Such letter must (i) be dated within three months of the subscription date, (ii) identify the specific basis for accredited investor qualification, and (iii) confirm that the verifier has taken reasonable steps to verify the investor's status.

## 6. Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

### 6.1 Triggers for Enhanced Due Diligence

Enhanced Due Diligence is required for any prospective investor or investment that meets one or more of the following criteria:

- Investor is classified as Medium-High or High risk per Section 3.2
- Investor is or has been a Politically Exposed Person (PEP)
- Investor is a foreign person or entity (even if U.S. tax resident)
- Investor is located in or has significant ties to a FATF-identified high-risk jurisdiction
- Any red flag identified per Section 3.3
- Funds originate from a foreign bank account
- Investment is made through a complex multi-entity structure
- Adverse information identified during standard screening

### 6.2 Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)

A PEP is defined as any individual who is or has been entrusted with a prominent public function, including:

- Heads of state or government, senior politicians, senior government officials
- Senior judicial or military officials
- Senior executives of state-owned enterprises
- Important political party officials
- Family members and close associates of the above

**EDD Measures for PEPs:**

| Measure | Description |
|---|---|
| Senior management approval | Investment acceptance requires approval from the CEO |
| Source of wealth verification | Independent verification of the PEP's source of wealth |
| Source of funds verification | Documentation of the specific source of investment funds |
| Enhanced ongoing monitoring | Annual re-screening and periodic review of relationship |
| Adverse media screening | Comprehensive media search for negative or concerning information |

### 6.3 Entity-Level Enhanced Due Diligence

For entities subject to EDD, the following additional procedures apply:

| Measure | Description |
|---|---|
| Full beneficial ownership chain | Identification of all beneficial owners through all layers of ownership to the ultimate natural person(s) |
| Entity purpose inquiry | Written explanation of the entity's business purpose and rationale for investment |
| Formation jurisdiction analysis | Assessment of the formation jurisdiction against FATF and Transparency International risk ratings |
| Financial statements review | Review of the entity's most recent financial statements (audited, if available) |
| Authorized signatory verification | In-person or video verification of the authorized signatory's identity |

### 6.4 High-Risk Jurisdiction List

The Company maintains a list of high-risk jurisdictions based on the following sources:

- FATF High-Risk Jurisdictions Subject to a Call for Action ("Blacklist")
- FATF Jurisdictions Under Increased Monitoring ("Grey List")
- OFAC Comprehensive Sanctions Programs
- EU High-Risk Third Countries List
- Transparency International Corruption Perceptions Index (bottom quartile)

Investments from or connected to jurisdictions on the FATF Blacklist or subject to OFAC comprehensive sanctions will not be accepted.

## 7. OFAC Screening Procedures

### 7.1 Overview

The U.S. Department of the Treasury's Office of Foreign Assets Control ("OFAC") administers and enforces economic and trade sanctions based on U.S. foreign policy and national security goals. Compliance with OFAC sanctions is mandatory for all U.S. persons and entities.

### 7.2 Screening Requirements

The Company will screen all prospective investors and their beneficial owners against the following OFAC lists prior to acceptance of any subscription:

| List | Description | Update Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons List (SDN List) | Individuals and entities whose assets are blocked | Continuously updated |
| Sectoral Sanctions Identifications (SSI) List | Entities operating in identified sectors of sanctioned countries | As amended |
| Foreign Sanctions Evaders (FSE) List | Persons subject to secondary sanctions for facilitating sanctions evasion | As amended |
| Non-SDN Palestinian Legislative Council (NS-PLC) List | Members of the Palestinian Legislative Council | As amended |
| Non-SDN Menu-Based Sanctions (NS-MBS) List | Persons subject to non-blocking menu-based sanctions | As amended |
| Consolidated Sanctions List | Combined list of all OFAC sanctions programs | Continuously updated |

### 7.3 Screening Procedure

1. **Initial Screening:** All prospective investors are screened at the time of subscription document submission
2. **Beneficial Owner Screening:** All identified beneficial owners of entity investors are individually screened
3. **Fuzzy Match Protocol:** The screening system uses fuzzy matching algorithms to identify potential matches (name variations, transliterations, aliases)
4. **Potential Match Review:** Any potential match is escalated to the Compliance Officer for manual review and resolution
5. **Confirmed Match Protocol:** If a confirmed match is identified:
   - The subscription is immediately rejected
   - No funds are accepted or, if already received, are frozen pending OFAC guidance
   - The Company contacts OFAC's compliance hotline: (800) 540-6322
   - Legal counsel is engaged
6. **False Positive Documentation:** False positives are documented with the basis for the determination

### 7.4 Country-Based Screening

The Company will not accept investments from persons or entities located in, organized under the laws of, or acting on behalf of persons in countries subject to comprehensive OFAC sanctions programs, including but not limited to:

- Cuba
- Iran
- North Korea
- Syria
- The Crimea, Donetsk, and Luhansk regions of Ukraine
- Russia (comprehensive sanctions apply to certain sectors and persons)

### 7.5 Rescreening

OFAC screening is repeated:

- At each closing or capital call
- Annually for all existing investors
- Upon OFAC list update notification for material changes
- Prior to any distribution to investors

## 8. Suspicious Activity Monitoring

### 8.1 Ongoing Monitoring Program

The Company maintains an ongoing monitoring program to detect suspicious activity throughout the life of the investment. Monitoring includes:

| Activity | Frequency | Responsible Party |
|---|---|---|
| Investor transaction monitoring | Each transaction | Compliance Officer |
| Unusual distribution requests | As they occur | Compliance Officer |
| Adverse media monitoring | Quarterly | Compliance Officer |
| OFAC list rescreening | Annually + material updates | Compliance Officer |
| Investor information updates | Annually | Compliance Officer |

### 8.2 Indicators of Suspicious Activity

The following non-exhaustive list of indicators may suggest suspicious activity requiring further investigation:

- Investor provides false or misleading identification information
- Investor attempts to structure investments to avoid reporting thresholds
- Investor requests distributions be sent to unrelated third parties
- Investor has no apparent legitimate business reason for the investment
- Investor funds are received from an unexpected source or jurisdiction
- Investor seeks to rapidly exit the investment shortly after entry
- Investor is the subject of law enforcement or regulatory inquiry
- Significant adverse media coverage regarding the investor
- Investor or beneficial owner appears on updated sanctions lists
- Investor refuses to provide updated information when reasonably requested

### 8.3 Investigation Procedures

Upon identification of a suspicious activity indicator:

1. The Compliance Officer documents the indicator and all relevant facts
2. The Compliance Officer conducts a preliminary investigation, including review of investor file and any additional screening
3. If the preliminary investigation does not resolve the concern, the matter is escalated to legal counsel
4. The Compliance Officer, in consultation with legal counsel, determines whether a SAR filing is warranted (see Section 12)
5. The investigation and its outcome are documented and maintained in the compliance file

## 12. Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) Filing Procedures

### 12.1 Filing Determination

While the Company is not currently classified as a financial institution required to file SARs under the BSA, the Company may voluntarily file a SAR with FinCEN if it identifies activity that it knows, suspects, or has reason to suspect:

- Involves funds derived from illegal activity or is intended to disguise such funds
- Is designed to evade reporting or record-keeping requirements
- Lacks a lawful purpose or is not the sort in which the particular investor would normally be expected to engage
- Involves use of the Company to facilitate criminal activity

### 12.2 Filing Procedures

If the Compliance Officer determines that a SAR filing is warranted:

1. Legal counsel is consulted prior to filing
2. FinCEN SAR is completed via the BSA E-Filing System (https://bsaefiling.fincen.treas.gov)
3. The SAR is filed within 30 calendar days of the determination
4. A copy of the SAR and all supporting documentation is maintained in a confidential, restricted-access file
5. **No disclosure:** The existence of a SAR filing shall not be disclosed to the subject of the report or any unauthorized person (31 U.S.C. § 5318(g)(2))

### 12.3 Law Enforcement Requests

The Company will cooperate with law enforcement requests for information, subject to legal counsel review and applicable legal requirements. Subpoenas and other legal process shall be directed to legal counsel.
