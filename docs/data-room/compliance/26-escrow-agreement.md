## 2. Recitals and Background

### 2.1 The Offering

**WHEREAS**, Abundancia Austin LLC (the "Company") is a Texas limited liability company formed for the purpose of developing, constructing, and operating a regenerative luxury community on approximately 120 acres of land located in Bastrop County, Texas (the "Project");

**WHEREAS**, Light Brands Consulting LLC serves as the Managing Member of the Company, with Nicholas Lawless serving as Manager;

**WHEREAS**, the Company is conducting a private offering (the "Offering") of membership interest units (the "Units") in the Company pursuant to Regulation D, Rule 506(c) promulgated under the Securities Act of 1933, as amended (the "Securities Act");

**WHEREAS**, the Offering contemplates the sale of up to two hundred fifty (250) Units at a purchase price of Fifty Thousand Dollars ($50,000.00) per Unit, for a maximum aggregate offering amount of Twelve Million Five Hundred Thousand Dollars ($12,500,000.00);

**WHEREAS**, the Offering is limited exclusively to verified accredited investors as defined in Rule 501(a) of Regulation D and verified in accordance with Rule 506(c);

**WHEREAS**, the parties desire to establish an escrow arrangement to hold subscription funds pending satisfaction of the conditions set forth herein.

### 2.2 Purpose of Escrow

**NOW, THEREFORE**, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows:

The purpose of this escrow arrangement is to:

1. **Protect investor capital** by holding subscription funds in a segregated, FDIC-insured account until closing conditions are satisfied
2. **Ensure regulatory compliance** with the requirements of Regulation D, Rule 506(c), and applicable state securities laws
3. **Establish transparent procedures** for the receipt, investment, release, and potential return of subscription funds
4. **Provide independent oversight** through a qualified, disinterested third-party escrow agent

## 4. Establishment of Escrow Account

### 4.1 Account Establishment

Within five (5) Business Days of the Effective Date, the Escrow Agent shall establish a segregated escrow account (the "Escrow Account") at [Escrow Agent Name] for the benefit of the investors in the Offering. The Escrow Account shall be:

| Requirement | Specification |
|-------------|---------------|
| Account Type | Segregated, interest-bearing demand deposit account |
| Account Title | "Abundancia Austin LLC -- Regulation D Offering Escrow Account" |
| Tax ID | The Company's Employer Identification Number (EIN): [________] |
| FDIC Insurance | Maintained at an FDIC-insured institution; aggregate balances in excess of FDIC limits to be managed in accordance with Section 4.3 |
| Segregation | Funds shall not be commingled with the Escrow Agent's own funds or with funds held in escrow for other parties |

### 4.2 Account Control

The Escrow Agent shall maintain sole and exclusive control over the Escrow Account at all times during the Escrow Period. Neither the Company, the Managing Member, nor any Investor shall have the right to withdraw, transfer, pledge, encumber, or otherwise exercise control over the Escrow Funds except as expressly provided in this Agreement.

### 4.3 FDIC Insurance and Excess Deposits

In the event that the aggregate balance of the Escrow Account exceeds the applicable FDIC insurance limit (currently $250,000 per depositor, per insured bank, per ownership category), the Escrow Agent shall:

1. Promptly notify the Company of the excess amount
2. At the Company's written direction, distribute excess funds among additional FDIC-insured deposit accounts at qualified banking institutions to maximize FDIC coverage (an "IntraFi" or similar deposit network arrangement)
3. Maintain records of all such distributed deposits and provide the Company with a consolidated statement of all Escrow Funds held

### 4.4 Pass-Through Insurance

The Escrow Agent shall maintain records sufficient to support FDIC pass-through insurance treatment, whereby each Investor's proportionate share of the Escrow Funds may be insured up to $250,000, provided that:

1. The Escrow Agent maintains records identifying each Investor's ownership interest in the Escrow Funds
2. The Escrow Account is established in a manner consistent with FDIC rules regarding pass-through insurance (12 C.F.R. Part 330)
3. The Company cooperates with the Escrow Agent in providing such information as may be necessary to support pass-through insurance treatment

### 4.5 Account Records and Statements

The Escrow Agent shall maintain complete and accurate records of all transactions in the Escrow Account, including:

- Date and amount of each deposit
- Identity of each depositing Investor
- Date and amount of each release or return
- Interest accrued and credited
- Fees deducted

The Escrow Agent shall provide monthly account statements to the Company within ten (10) Business Days following the end of each calendar month.

## 5. Deposit Procedures

### 5.1 Accepted Forms of Payment

Subscription payments shall be accepted in the following forms:

| Payment Method | Processing Time | Requirements |
|----------------|-----------------|--------------|
| Domestic Wire Transfer | Same-day to next Business Day | Must reference Investor name and subscription number |
| International Wire Transfer | 2-3 Business Days | Must reference Investor name; subject to enhanced AML review |
| ACH Transfer | 2-3 Business Days | Pre-authorized only; must reference Investor name |
| Certified Check / Cashier's Check | Subject to clearing | Payable to "Abundancia Austin LLC Escrow Account c/o [Escrow Agent Name]" |

The following payment methods are **not accepted**:

| Prohibited Method | Reason |
|-------------------|--------|
| Personal checks | Insufficient verification and clearing risk |
| Cash deposits | AML/KYC concerns; lack of audit trail |
| Cryptocurrency | Regulatory uncertainty; valuation complexity |
| Third-party payments | Source-of-funds verification requirements |
| Money orders | Insufficient verification and fraud risk |

### 5.2 Wire Transfer Instructions

Wire transfer instructions for the Escrow Account are set forth in **Exhibit A** attached hereto. The Company shall provide these instructions to each Investor solely through the Subscription Agreement or through direct communication from the Company's authorized representative. Wire instructions shall specify:

- Escrow Agent bank name and address
- ABA routing number
- Account number (Escrow Account)
- Account name: "Abundancia Austin LLC -- Regulation D Offering Escrow Account"
- Reference: Subscriber name and subscription number
- SWIFT code (for international wires)

### 5.3 Deposit Documentation

Each deposit into the Escrow Account must be accompanied by or preceded by:

1. A fully executed Subscription Agreement
2. Completed Investor Questionnaire and Accredited Investor Certification
3. Verification of accredited investor status in accordance with Rule 506(c) procedures
4. Completed W-9 (for U.S. persons) or applicable W-8 form (for non-U.S. persons)
5. Government-issued photo identification (as required by AML/KYC procedures)
6. Source-of-funds documentation (if required by enhanced due diligence procedures)

### 5.4 Deposit Acknowledgment

Within three (3) Business Days of receipt of subscription funds into the Escrow Account, the Escrow Agent shall:

1. Confirm receipt of funds to the Company in writing (which may be by electronic communication)
2. Provide the Company with the depositing Investor's name, amount received, and date of deposit

The Company shall, within five (5) Business Days of receiving confirmation from the Escrow Agent, provide written acknowledgment of receipt to the Investor, including:

- Confirmation of amount received
- Date of receipt
- Number of Units subscribed
- Status of subscription (pending review)
- Estimated timeline for closing or return

### 5.5 Rejected Deposits

The Escrow Agent shall reject any deposit that:

1. Is not accompanied by proper documentation as described in Section 5.3
2. Is received from a source that does not match the subscribing Investor's identity
3. Is made by a method not authorized under Section 5.1
4. Raises concerns under applicable AML/KYC procedures
5. Is received after the expiration of the Offering Period

Rejected funds shall be returned to the originating account within five (5) Business Days, without interest, and the Company shall be notified of the rejection and the reason therefor.

### 5.6 Running Escrow Ledger

The Escrow Agent shall maintain a running ledger (the "Escrow Ledger") documenting:

| Data Element | Description |
|--------------|-------------|
| Investor Name | Legal name of the subscribing Investor |
| Subscription Number | Unique identifier assigned by the Company |
| Deposit Date | Date funds are received and cleared in the Escrow Account |
| Deposit Amount | Dollar amount of subscription payment |
| Number of Units | Units subscribed based on deposit amount |
| Payment Method | Wire, ACH, or certified check |
| Cumulative Total | Running total of all cleared deposits |
| Status | Pending / Cleared / Released / Returned |

The Escrow Agent shall provide the Company with an updated Escrow Ledger on a weekly basis during the Offering Period, or more frequently upon reasonable written request.

### 5.7 Subscription Overages and Partial Subscriptions

In the event that a deposit amount does not correspond to an exact number of Units at $50,000.00 per Unit:

1. **Overage amounts** less than $50,000.00 shall be held in escrow and returned to the Investor at the time of Closing or release, unless the Investor provides supplemental funds to complete an additional Unit subscription
2. **Partial Unit subscriptions** are not permitted; all subscriptions must be in whole Units ($50,000.00 increments)
3. The Company shall notify the Investor of any discrepancy within five (5) Business Days and provide instructions for resolution

## 6. Investment of Escrow Funds

### 6.1 Permissible Investments

During the Escrow Period, the Escrow Agent shall invest and reinvest the Escrow Funds, at the written direction of the Company, in one or more of the following:

1. **FDIC-insured demand deposit accounts** at the Escrow Agent's institution or at other qualified banking institutions
2. **U.S. Treasury securities** with maturities not exceeding ninety (90) days
3. **Money market funds** investing exclusively in U.S. government obligations, rated no lower than AAAm by Standard & Poor's or Aaa-mf by Moody's
4. **Certificates of deposit** issued by FDIC-insured institutions with maturities not exceeding ninety (90) days

### 6.2 Investment Direction

In the absence of written investment instructions from the Company, the Escrow Agent shall hold Escrow Funds in the default interest-bearing demand deposit account established under Section 4.1.

### 6.3 Interest and Earnings

All interest and earnings on the Escrow Funds shall be credited to the Escrow Account and shall constitute part of the Escrow Funds. Interest and earnings shall be allocated among Investors on a pro rata basis in proportion to the amount and duration of each Investor's deposit.

### 6.4 Tax Reporting

Interest and earnings on the Escrow Funds shall be reported for federal, state, and local income tax purposes as income of the respective Investors. The Escrow Agent shall issue IRS Form 1099-INT or other applicable tax reporting forms to each Investor and to the Internal Revenue Service as required by law.

### 6.5 Investment Risk

The Company acknowledges that (a) the Escrow Agent shall not be liable for any loss of principal or interest resulting from market fluctuations in permissible investments, and (b) deposits in excess of FDIC insurance limits are subject to credit risk of the depository institution.

## 8. Minimum Raise Threshold

### 8.1 Threshold Amount

The Minimum Raise Threshold for this Offering is **Three Million Seven Hundred Fifty Thousand Dollars ($3,750,000.00)**, representing the subscription price for seventy-five (75) Units, or thirty percent (30%) of the Maximum Offering Amount.

### 8.2 Rationale

The Minimum Raise Threshold has been established to ensure that sufficient capital is raised to commence meaningful project development:

| Purpose | Estimated Allocation |
|---------|---------------------|
| Land acquisition and initial site development | $1,500,000 |
| Architectural and engineering design | $750,000 |
| Permitting, legal, and regulatory compliance | $500,000 |
| Project management and working capital | $500,000 |
| Contingency reserve | $500,000 |
| **Total Minimum Raise** | **$3,750,000** |

### 8.3 Threshold Deadline

The Minimum Raise Threshold must be achieved on or before [Threshold Deadline Date] (the "Threshold Deadline"). If the Minimum Raise Threshold is not met by the Threshold Deadline:

1. The Escrow Agent shall return all Escrow Funds to the respective Investors in accordance with Section 9
2. No portion of the Escrow Funds shall be released to the Company
3. This Agreement shall terminate automatically upon completion of all refunds

### 8.4 Extension of Threshold Deadline

The Threshold Deadline may be extended by up to ninety (90) days upon:

1. Written notice from the Company to the Escrow Agent, delivered no later than ten (10) Business Days prior to the original Threshold Deadline
2. Written notice from the Company to all existing Investors, informing them of the extension and their right to withdraw their subscription during a fifteen (15) day withdrawal window
3. Any Investor who elects to withdraw during the withdrawal window shall have their funds returned within ten (10) Business Days
4. A second extension of up to ninety (90) additional days may be granted under the same conditions, for a maximum total extension of one hundred eighty (180) days

### 8.5 Threshold Monitoring

The Escrow Agent shall provide the Company with a threshold status report on a weekly basis (or more frequently upon request) during the period leading up to the Threshold Deadline, indicating:

| Data Point | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| Aggregate Cleared Deposits | Total cleared funds in the Escrow Account |
| Number of Accepted Subscriptions | Count of Investors with cleared deposits |
| Units Subscribed | Total Units corresponding to cleared deposits |
| Percentage of Threshold | Cleared deposits as a percentage of $3,750,000 |
| Remaining to Threshold | Dollar amount needed to reach the Minimum Raise Threshold |
| Days Remaining | Business days until the Threshold Deadline |

## 9. Return of Funds Provisions

### 9.1 Mandatory Return Events

The Escrow Agent shall return Escrow Funds to the respective Investors upon the occurrence of any of the following events:

| Return Event | Description | Return Timeline |
|--------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Failure to Meet Minimum Raise Threshold** | The Minimum Raise Threshold is not met by the Threshold Deadline (as may be extended) | 15 Business Days |
| **Termination of the Offering** | The Company delivers written notice that the Offering has been terminated | 15 Business Days |
| **Expiration of the Offering Period** | The Offering Period expires without a Closing having occurred | 15 Business Days |
| **Rejection of Subscription** | The Company notifies the Escrow Agent that an Investor's subscription has been rejected | 10 Business Days |
| **Investor Withdrawal** | An Investor delivers a valid withdrawal notice during an applicable withdrawal period | 10 Business Days |
| **Regulatory Order** | A court or regulatory authority issues an order requiring the return of funds | As ordered |
| **Material Adverse Change** | The Company elects not to proceed following a Material Adverse Change | 15 Business Days |

### 9.2 Return Mechanics

Upon the occurrence of a Mandatory Return Event, the Escrow Agent shall:

1. Return Escrow Funds to each affected Investor within the applicable timeline set forth in Section 9.1
2. Return funds by wire transfer to the originating account or by check mailed to the Investor's address of record, as directed by the Company
3. Include pro rata interest earned on the returned funds from the date of deposit through the date of return
4. Deduct no fees or charges from the returned funds (all Escrow Agent fees shall be borne by the Company)
5. Provide the Company with a return statement identifying each Investor, the amount returned, and the interest included
6. Update the Escrow Ledger to reflect the return

### 9.3 Investor Withdrawal Rights

**Prior to Closing**, an Investor may withdraw their subscription and request return of funds by delivering written notice to both the Company and the Escrow Agent, subject to the following:

| Withdrawal Scenario | Notice Period | Return Timing |
|---------------------|---------------|---------------|
| During Offering Period (before Minimum Threshold met) | 10 Business Days' written notice | Within 10 Business Days after notice period expires |
| During Threshold Deadline extension period | 15 calendar days from receipt of extension notice | Within 10 Business Days after election |
| After Minimum Threshold met but before Closing | Permitted only if Closing has not occurred within 90 days of threshold being met | Within 10 Business Days after notice |
| Following material amendment to Offering terms | 30 calendar days from receipt of amendment notice | Within 10 Business Days after election |

### 9.4 Withdrawal Notice Requirements

A valid withdrawal notice must:

1. Be in writing, signed by the Investor (or authorized representative)
2. Identify the Investor by name and subscription number
3. State the amount to be withdrawn
4. Provide wire instructions for the return of funds (or confirm return to the originating account)
5. Be delivered to both the Company and the Escrow Agent

The form of withdrawal notice is attached as **Exhibit E**.

### 9.5 No Withdrawal After Closing

Once Escrow Funds have been released to the Company at Closing, Investors shall have no right to withdraw their subscription or demand return of funds from the Escrow Account. Post-closing redemption rights, if any, are governed solely by the Operating Agreement.

### 9.6 Unclaimed Funds

If the Escrow Agent is unable to return funds to an Investor after reasonable efforts (including at least two attempts at the last known address or wire instructions on file), the Escrow Agent shall:

1. Hold such unclaimed funds in the Escrow Account for a period of one hundred eighty (180) days
2. Send a final notice to the Investor's last known address by certified mail during the holding period
3. After such period, deliver the unclaimed funds to the Company, which shall hold such funds in trust for the benefit of the Investor and comply with the Texas Unclaimed Property Act (Tex. Prop. Code Chapter 72) and any other applicable unclaimed property laws

## 10. Escrow Agent Duties and Responsibilities

### 10.1 Ministerial Role

The Escrow Agent's duties under this Agreement are purely ministerial in nature. The Escrow Agent shall:

1. **Receive and hold** subscription funds deposited in accordance with this Agreement
2. **Maintain** the Escrow Account and Escrow Ledger as described herein
3. **Release or return** Escrow Funds only in accordance with the terms of this Agreement
4. **Provide** statements and reports as required by this Agreement
5. **Comply** with lawful orders of courts of competent jurisdiction and regulatory authorities
6. **Maintain** records in accordance with the document retention requirements of Section 19.7

### 10.2 No Investigation Duty

The Escrow Agent shall have no duty to:

1. Investigate or verify the truth, accuracy, or completeness of any document, certificate, or instruction delivered pursuant to this Agreement
2. Determine whether the Release Conditions have actually been satisfied (the Escrow Agent may rely conclusively on the Company's Release Certificate)
3. Monitor the conduct of the Offering or the Company's compliance with securities laws
4. Evaluate the merits of the investment or the creditworthiness of any party
5. Verify the accredited investor status of any Investor
6. Determine the validity of any Subscription Agreement or other Offering Document
7. Advise any party as to the tax consequences of the escrow arrangement

### 10.3 Reliance on Instructions

The Escrow Agent shall be entitled to rely upon, and shall be protected in acting or refraining from acting upon, any written notice, instruction, certificate, or document that the Escrow Agent in good faith believes to be genuine, to have been signed or presented by the proper party or parties, and to conform to the requirements of this Agreement.

### 10.4 Conflicting Instructions

In the event the Escrow Agent receives conflicting instructions from the Company and any other party:

1. The Escrow Agent shall promptly notify all relevant parties of the conflict
2. The Escrow Agent may, in its sole discretion, refrain from taking any action until the conflict is resolved
3. The Escrow Agent may interplead the Escrow Funds into a court of competent jurisdiction and shall thereupon be released from further liability, except for the obligation to account for all Escrow Funds held

### 10.5 Compliance with Legal Process

The Escrow Agent shall comply with any writ, order, judgment, or decree of a court of competent jurisdiction or any order of a governmental regulatory authority, including but not limited to levies, garnishments, attachments, and tax liens. The Escrow Agent shall promptly notify the Company of any such legal process.

### 10.6 Escrow Agent's Own Counsel

The Escrow Agent may, at any time, consult with its own legal counsel regarding any matter arising under this Agreement, and the Escrow Agent shall not be liable for any action taken or omitted in good faith in accordance with the advice of such counsel.
