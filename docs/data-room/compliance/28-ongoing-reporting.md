## 1. Overview and Purpose

### 1.1 Purpose

This Ongoing Reporting Framework establishes the comprehensive reporting obligations, communication protocols, and disclosure commitments of Abundancia Austin LLC (the "Company") to its membership interest unit holders (the "Investors" or "Members"). This framework is designed to ensure transparency, regulatory compliance, and timely dissemination of material information throughout the life of the investment.

### 1.2 Offering Context

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Issuer | Abundancia Austin LLC (Texas LLC) |
| Managing Member | Light Brands Consulting LLC |
| Manager | Nicholas Lawless |
| Securities | Membership Interest Units (250 Units at $50,000 each) |
| Total Raise | $12,500,000.00 |
| Regulation | Regulation D, Rule 506(c) |
| Project | Regenerative luxury community, 120 acres, Bastrop County, Texas |

### 1.3 Reporting Commitments

The Company commits to the following reporting standards:

| Commitment | Standard |
|------------|----------|
| **Transparency** | All material information affecting the value of the investment or the rights of Members shall be disclosed promptly |
| **Timeliness** | All reports shall be delivered within the deadlines specified in this framework |
| **Accuracy** | All financial information shall be prepared in accordance with Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP) or such other basis as disclosed |
| **Consistency** | Reporting format and content shall be consistent from period to period to facilitate investor analysis |
| **Accessibility** | All reports shall be available through the Investor Portal and delivered via the communication methods described herein |
| **Compliance** | All reporting shall comply with the Operating Agreement, applicable securities laws, and tax regulations |

### 1.4 Governing Documents

This reporting framework is established pursuant to and consistent with:

1. The Amended and Restated Operating Agreement of Abundancia Austin LLC (the "Operating Agreement")
2. The Confidential Private Placement Memorandum (the "PPM")
3. Regulation D, Rule 506(c) under the Securities Act of 1933

In the event of any conflict between this framework and the Operating Agreement, the Operating Agreement shall control.

## 2. Quarterly Investor Reports

### 2.1 Overview

The Company shall prepare and distribute quarterly investor reports (the "Quarterly Reports") within forty-five (45) days following the end of each fiscal quarter. Fiscal quarters end on March 31, June 30, September 30, and December 31.

### 2.2 Quarterly Report Schedule

| Quarter | Period End | Report Due | Distribution Method |
|---------|-----------|------------|---------------------|
| Q1 | March 31 | May 15 | Investor Portal + Email |
| Q2 | June 30 | August 14 | Investor Portal + Email |
| Q3 | September 30 | November 14 | Investor Portal + Email |
| Q4 | December 31 | February 14 | Investor Portal + Email |

### 2.3 Quarterly Report Content

Each Quarterly Report shall contain the following sections:

**Section A: Executive Summary**

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Manager's Letter | Narrative overview of the quarter's activities, accomplishments, and challenges |
| Key Metrics Dashboard | Summary of critical financial and operational metrics |
| Project Status | High-level project development status (pre-development, construction, operations) |
| Outlook | Forward-looking commentary on upcoming milestones and anticipated activities |

**Section B: Financial Statements (Unaudited)**

| Statement | Content |
|-----------|---------|
| Balance Sheet | Assets, liabilities, and members' equity as of quarter-end |
| Income Statement | Revenue, expenses, and net income for the quarter and year-to-date |
| Cash Flow Statement | Operating, investing, and financing cash flows for the quarter and year-to-date |
| Capital Account Summary | Each Member's capital account balance as of quarter-end, including contributions, distributions, and allocated income/loss |

**Section C: Operational Report**

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Development Progress | Status of construction, permits, inspections, and milestones |
| Budget vs. Actual | Comparison of actual expenditures to the approved project budget |
| Timeline Update | Updated project timeline with key milestone dates |
| Vendor and Contractor Summary | Status of key vendor and contractor relationships |
| Regulatory Compliance | Summary of permit status, inspection results, and regulatory interactions |

**Section D: Investment Performance**

| Metric | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Cash-on-Cash Return | Annual cash distributions as a percentage of invested capital |
| Internal Rate of Return (IRR) | Projected and actual IRR based on cash flows to date |
| Equity Multiple | Projected total return multiple on invested capital |
| Net Asset Value (NAV) | Estimated NAV per Unit based on current project valuation |
| Distribution History | Cumulative distributions per Unit to date |

**Section E: Risk and Compliance**

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Risk Register Update | Summary of material risks, changes in risk profile, and mitigation measures |
| Legal and Regulatory | Summary of any legal proceedings, regulatory actions, or compliance matters |
| Insurance Status | Confirmation that all required insurance policies are current |
| Bad Actor Monitoring | Confirmation that no disqualifying events have occurred under Rule 506(d) |

### 2.4 Quarterly Report Format

Quarterly Reports shall be delivered in PDF format via the Investor Portal and email. The format shall be consistent from quarter to quarter and shall include:

1. Professional formatting with Company branding
2. Table of contents with hyperlinked sections
3. Charts and graphs for key metrics where appropriate
4. Footnotes explaining significant variances or assumptions
5. Contact information for investor inquiries

### 2.5 First-Year Reporting Adjustments

During the first fiscal year of operations (which may include a partial first quarter), the Company may adjust the scope of quarterly reporting as follows:

| Phase | Reporting Scope |
|-------|----------------|
| Pre-Development | Executive summary, budget status, timeline, capital account balances |
| Development / Construction | Full quarterly report as described above, with emphasis on construction progress |
| Operations | Full quarterly report with emphasis on revenue, occupancy, and distributions |

## 3. Annual Financial Statements

### 3.1 Overview

The Company shall prepare annual financial statements (the "Annual Financial Statements") for each fiscal year. The fiscal year ends on December 31.

### 3.2 Annual Report Schedule

| Deliverable | Due Date | Distribution |
|-------------|----------|-------------|
| Unaudited Annual Financial Statements | March 1 following fiscal year-end | Investor Portal + Email |
| Audited Annual Financial Statements (if required) | April 15 following fiscal year-end | Investor Portal + Email |
| Annual Letter from Manager | March 1 following fiscal year-end | Investor Portal + Email |

### 3.3 Financial Statement Content

The Annual Financial Statements shall include:

| Statement | Basis | Content |
|-----------|-------|---------|
| Balance Sheet | GAAP (or tax basis, as disclosed) | Assets, liabilities, and members' equity |
| Statement of Operations | GAAP (or tax basis) | Revenue, cost of revenue, operating expenses, net income/loss |
| Statement of Changes in Members' Equity | GAAP (or tax basis) | Capital contributions, distributions, allocated income/loss, ending balances |
| Statement of Cash Flows | GAAP (or tax basis) | Operating, investing, and financing activities |
| Notes to Financial Statements | GAAP disclosure requirements | Significant accounting policies, commitments, contingencies, related party transactions, subsequent events |

### 3.4 Audit Requirements

| Condition | Audit Standard |
|-----------|---------------|
| First fiscal year (pre-revenue) | Compilation or review by independent CPA acceptable |
| Second fiscal year and thereafter | Review engagement by independent CPA (minimum) |
| If total assets exceed $25,000,000 | Full audit by independent CPA (AICPA standards) |
| If required by Operating Agreement | As specified in the Operating Agreement |
| If requested by Members holding >25% of Units | Full audit at Company expense |

### 3.5 Accounting Basis

The Company intends to maintain its books and records on the [accrual / cash / tax] basis of accounting. The Annual Financial Statements shall clearly disclose the accounting basis used. If the tax basis is used, reconciliation to GAAP shall be provided upon request.

### 3.6 Independent CPA

The Company shall engage an independent certified public accounting firm (the "Independent CPA") to prepare or review the Annual Financial Statements. The Independent CPA shall:

1. Be licensed in the State of Texas
2. Maintain professional liability insurance
3. Have experience with real estate development entities and partnership tax reporting
4. Be independent of the Company, the Managing Member, and the Manager

### 3.7 Annual Manager's Letter

The Annual Manager's Letter shall include:

1. Comprehensive review of the fiscal year's activities and accomplishments
2. Comparison of actual performance to projections contained in the PPM
3. Discussion of significant challenges and how they were addressed
4. Forward-looking commentary on the upcoming fiscal year
5. Summary of capital expenditures and budget performance
6. Discussion of any changes in strategy, key personnel, or material relationships
7. Summary of all distributions made during the year
8. Projected timeline for key milestones in the upcoming year

## 4. Schedule K-1 Distribution

### 4.1 Overview

As a multi-member limited liability company taxed as a partnership, the Company is required to issue IRS Schedule K-1 (Form 1065, Partner's Share of Income, Deductions, Credits, etc.) to each Member annually.

### 4.2 K-1 Distribution Schedule

| Deliverable | Due Date | Distribution Method |
|-------------|----------|---------------------|
| Estimated K-1 Information (for tax planning) | February 15 | Email + Investor Portal |
| Final Schedule K-1 | March 15 (or upon filing of partnership return) | Email + Investor Portal + U.S. Mail |
| Amended K-1 (if necessary) | Promptly upon determination of amendment | Email + Investor Portal + U.S. Mail |

### 4.3 K-1 Content

Each Schedule K-1 shall reflect the Member's allocable share of:

| Item | K-1 Line | Description |
|------|----------|-------------|
| Ordinary Business Income (Loss) | Line 1 | Member's share of the Company's ordinary income or loss |
| Net Rental Real Estate Income (Loss) | Line 2 | Member's share of rental income or loss |
| Other Net Rental Income (Loss) | Line 3 | Other rental activity income or loss |
| Guaranteed Payments | Line 4 | Guaranteed payments for services or use of capital (if applicable) |
| Interest Income | Line 5 | Member's share of interest income |
| Dividends | Line 6 | Member's share of dividends (if any) |
| Royalties | Line 7 | Member's share of royalties (if any) |
| Net Short-Term Capital Gain (Loss) | Line 8 | Short-term capital gains or losses |
| Net Long-Term Capital Gain (Loss) | Line 9a | Long-term capital gains or losses |
| Section 1231 Gain (Loss) | Line 10 | Gains or losses on sale of business property |
| Other Income (Loss) | Line 11 | Other items of income or loss |
| Section 179 Deduction | Line 12 | Section 179 expense deduction |
| Other Deductions | Line 13 | Other deductible items |
| Self-Employment Earnings | Line 14 | Self-employment income (if applicable) |
| Credits | Line 15 | Tax credits (e.g., rehabilitation, energy credits) |
| Alternative Minimum Tax Items | Line 17 | AMT adjustments and preferences |
| Tax-Exempt Income | Line 18 | Tax-exempt interest and other exempt income |
| Distributions | Line 19 | Cash and property distributions during the year |
| Other Information | Line 20 | Additional information including Section 199A QBI deduction information |

### 4.4 Tax Basis and Capital Account Reporting

Beginning in tax year 2020, the IRS requires partnerships to report partners' capital accounts on the tax basis method on Schedule K-1. The Company shall:

1. Maintain each Member's capital account on both GAAP and tax basis
2. Report the tax basis capital account on Schedule K-1 (Item L)
3. Provide GAAP capital account information in the quarterly and annual reports
4. Track each Member's outside basis for purposes of loss limitation and distribution analysis (information provided upon request)

### 4.5 State Tax Reporting

The Company may be required to file state partnership returns and issue state K-1 equivalents in states where it conducts business or where Members reside. Anticipated state filings include:

| State | Filing | K-1 Equivalent | Notes |
|-------|--------|-----------------|-------|
| Texas | Texas Franchise Tax (Form 05-102) | No state K-1; margin tax return | Texas has no personal income tax |
| [Other states where Members reside] | State partnership return | State K-1 or equivalent | Filed as required based on Member residency |

### 4.6 K-1 Inquiries

Members with questions about their Schedule K-1 should contact:

1. The Company's designated tax reporting contact (identified in the Investor Portal)
2. Their own tax advisor for guidance on reporting K-1 items on their personal tax returns

The Company will make reasonable efforts to respond to K-1 inquiries within ten (10) business days.

### 4.7 Extension of Partnership Return

If the Company files an extension for the partnership tax return (Form 7004), the K-1 distribution timeline will be adjusted as follows:

| Scenario | Estimated K-1 | Final K-1 |
|----------|---------------|-----------|
| No extension | February 15 | March 15 |
| Extension filed | February 15 | September 15 (extended deadline) |

The Company will notify Members promptly if an extension is filed and provide the estimated K-1 information by February 15 regardless of extension status.

## 5. Material Event Notifications

### 5.1 Overview

The Company shall promptly notify all Members of the occurrence of any Material Event as defined below. Material Event Notifications shall be delivered within the timeframes specified in this section.

### 5.2 Definition of Material Events

A "Material Event" is any event, occurrence, or development that a reasonable investor would consider significant in evaluating their investment, including but not limited to:

**Category A: Financial Events (Notification within 5 Business Days)**

| Event | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Material deviation from budget | Actual costs exceeding budget by 15% or more for any major line item or 10% in aggregate |
| Impairment of assets | Determination that project assets have suffered material impairment |
| Liquidity event | Inability to meet financial obligations within 30 days |
| Loan default or acceleration | Default on any debt obligation or acceleration of any loan |
| Capital call | Determination that additional capital contributions are required |
| Insurance claim | Filing of any insurance claim exceeding $250,000 |

**Category B: Legal and Regulatory Events (Notification within 5 Business Days)**

| Event | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Litigation | Filing or receipt of any lawsuit involving the Company with claimed damages exceeding $100,000 |
| Regulatory action | Receipt of any cease-and-desist order, subpoena, investigation notice, or enforcement action from any regulatory authority |
| Environmental notice | Receipt of any notice of environmental violation, contamination, or remediation requirement |
| Permit revocation or denial | Revocation, denial, or material modification of any permit required for the Project |
| Tax audit | Receipt of notice of audit by the IRS or any state tax authority |
| Bad actor event | Occurrence of any disqualifying event under Rule 506(d) with respect to any covered person |

**Category C: Operational Events (Notification within 10 Business Days)**

| Event | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Key personnel change | Departure or incapacity of the Manager or any key member of the development team |
| Contractor default | Default, termination, or insolvency of any contractor performing work exceeding $500,000 |
| Force majeure | Occurrence of any force majeure event causing project delay exceeding 30 days |
| Material design change | Significant change in project design, scope, or specifications affecting project economics |
| Zoning or entitlement change | Any change in zoning, entitlement, or land use approval affecting the Project |
| Casualty or condemnation | Any damage to or condemnation of the Project property |

**Category D: Structural Events (Notification within 5 Business Days)**

| Event | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Change in Managing Member | Replacement, removal, or change in Light Brands Consulting LLC as Managing Member |
| Change in Manager | Replacement or removal of Nicholas Lawless as Manager |
| Amendment to Operating Agreement | Any proposed amendment to the Operating Agreement |
| Related party transaction | Any transaction between the Company and the Manager, Managing Member, or their affiliates exceeding $50,000 |
| Transfer of interest | Any transfer, assignment, or encumbrance of a Member's interest exceeding 10% of total Units |
| Dissolution or winding up | Determination to dissolve, wind up, or liquidate the Company |

### 5.3 Notification Procedures

Material Event Notifications shall be delivered as follows:

| Priority | Delivery Method | Timeline |
|----------|----------------|----------|
| **Urgent** (Category A financial distress, Category D structural) | Email + telephone to all Members | Within 2 Business Days |
| **Standard** (All other Category A, B, D events) | Email + Investor Portal posting | Within 5 Business Days |
| **Informational** (Category C operational events) | Email + Investor Portal posting | Within 10 Business Days |

### 5.4 Content of Material Event Notifications

Each Material Event Notification shall include:

1. Clear identification of the event and the date of occurrence
2. Factual description of the event and its circumstances
3. Assessment of the impact on the Project, the Company, and Members' investment
4. Description of actions taken or planned by management in response
5. Timeline for resolution or further updates
6. Contact information for Member inquiries
7. Statement that the notification does not constitute legal or tax advice

### 5.5 Follow-Up Reporting

Following a Material Event Notification, the Company shall provide:

1. Status updates at least every thirty (30) days until the event is resolved
2. A final resolution report upon conclusion of the matter
3. Discussion of the event and its resolution in the next Quarterly Report

All digital records shall be maintained in accordance with the following standards:

| Standard | Requirement |

### 9.4 Physical Storage Standards

Physical records shall be maintained in:

1. A secure, fire-resistant storage facility or safe
2. Climate-controlled environment to prevent deterioration
3. Restricted-access location with access log

### 9.5 Destruction Procedures

Upon expiration of the applicable retention period, documents may be destroyed subject to the following:

1. No document shall be destroyed if it is subject to a litigation hold, regulatory investigation, or audit
2. Physical documents shall be shredded using cross-cut shredding
3. Digital documents shall be permanently deleted from all storage media, including backups
4. A destruction log shall be maintained documenting the date, description, and method of destruction
5. The Manager shall approve all destruction requests

### 9.6 Litigation Hold

Upon learning of any pending or threatened litigation, investigation, or audit, the Company shall immediately implement a litigation hold, suspending all routine document destruction for records that may be relevant to the matter. The litigation hold shall remain in effect until the matter is resolved and the hold is formally lifted by the Manager in consultation with legal counsel.

## 15. Amendment and Update Procedures

### 15.1 Amendment Authority

This Ongoing Reporting Framework may be amended by the Manager at any time, provided that:

1. No amendment shall reduce the frequency or scope of reporting below the minimum standards set forth in the Operating Agreement
2. No amendment shall reduce the frequency of reporting below quarterly for financial information or annually for audited/reviewed statements
3. Members shall be notified of any material amendment at least thirty (30) days prior to the effective date
4. Amendments expanding reporting obligations may be implemented immediately

### 15.2 Annual Review

The Manager shall review this Ongoing Reporting Framework at least annually to:

1. Assess whether reporting obligations are being met
2. Identify opportunities to improve the quality or timeliness of reporting
3. Incorporate changes in regulatory requirements
4. Respond to Member feedback regarding reporting
5. Update filing calendars and deadlines

### 15.3 Regulatory Updates

If changes in applicable law, regulation, or SEC guidance require modifications to the Company's reporting obligations, the Manager shall:

1. Implement the required changes promptly
2. Notify Members of the change and its impact
3. Update this framework accordingly
4. Consult with securities counsel and the Independent CPA as appropriate

Members are encouraged to provide feedback on the Company's reporting practices. Feedback may be submitted through the Investor Portal contact feature or by email to the Manager. The Manager shall consider Member feedback in connection with the annual review of this framework.
